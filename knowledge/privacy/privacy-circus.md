# 181557496.Privacy Circus

The market’s bleeding again, Bitcoin’s down another few percent like it’s allergic to weekends, and here I am doomscrolling Crypto Twitter, watching everyone lose their minds over Zcash. Again. That dusty old privacy coin we all wrote off years ago is suddenly the hottest thing since Solana in 2021, with shielded pools hitting all-time highs—around 30% of the supply locked away in proper encryption—and big names piling in like it’s 2017 all over.

Arthur Hayes is out there calling it his family’s second-biggest holding after Bitcoin, throwing around $10k targets like confetti. Naval Ravikant—yeah, the guy who usually drops zen wisdom—labeled it “insurance against Bitcoin.” Mert Mumtaz from Helius is shilling it nonstop, wearing Zcash hats at conferences and calling privacy a moral imperative.

## Context

Price went berserk: from summer scraps around $50 to a ridiculous $744 peak in early November after the halving, then puked 40% down to the $440-ish range now. Still up insane YTD, while everything else is nursing hangovers.

You feel that itch, right? That FOMO mixed with “wait, haven’t we done this before?” I’ve been grinding in this space for over a decade—back when Bitcoin was forum nerd money—and I’ve seen every flavor of hype. ICOs, DeFi summer, NFTs, you name it.

Here’s the thing that still haunts me: Berlin, 2014. Dim basement bar, too much beer, some sketchy dude pitching this “ultimate anonymous coin.” I threw in rent money—yeah, actual rent—watched it 10x overnight on pump forum whispers, felt like a genius. Then poof, team vanished, wallet drained in a rug that wasn’t even clever. Mortifying. Taught me to spot the patterns, though.

## Main

The same breathless narratives, the same influencers suddenly “discovering” something that’s been sitting there forever.

So we’re chatting now, you and me, over this imaginary fancy French wine that’s gone warm. No slides, no bullshit optimism. Just venting.

Look, privacy’s always been the dirty secret of crypto. Obama nailed it back in 2016: strong encryption means everyone’s got a Swiss bank in their pocket. Governments hate that—rich folks already hide money offshore, why let the rest of us? Pandora Papers showed the hypocrisy plain as day.

But try real privacy? Backdoors, spyware, metadata leaks. Signal hands over who/when/where. Pegasus cracks the rest if you’re worth it.

Bitcoin? Solana? Everything’s naked on-chain. Great for “transparency,” terrible if you don’t want hackers, kidnappers, or the tax man knowing your balance.

Zcash was supposed to fix that—proper zk-SNARKs hiding sender, receiver, amount. Better than Monero’s obfuscation tricks. But regulators hammered it: delistings everywhere, bans in Asia, EU’s MiCA squeezing anonymous stuff hard. US is a mess, but no full ban yet—Winklevoss twins even donating ZEC to pro-freedom PACs.

Bans just push it underground. DEXs, intent swaps on NEAR, self-custody. Real uses too: abuse survivors hiding funds, journalists in shithole regimes, companies protecting trades.

And damn, in this crap market, Zcash mooned. Halving cut supply, shielded pool swelled—users actually shielding and holding, not flipping. Zashi wallet finally makes it easy, default private like it should be. NEAR lets you swap BTC straight to shielded ZEC, no KYC bullshit. Tachyon coming for faster private txs, Ztarknet for private DeFi someday. Grayscale hoarding, ETF rumors.

Influencers poured gas: Mert banging the “moral imperative” drum, Hayes with wild targets, Naval’s insurance line shifting the story from “drug coin” to “surveillance hedge” as CBDCs and AI creep in.

But wait. Skeptics calling it a coordinated pump—denied, but retail always eats the dip while whales skate. Distribution signs popping up.

Honestly, my cynicism kicks in hard here. Crypto started with transparent ledgers—no middlemen, verifiable trust. Now everyone’s chasing privacy maximalism? Feels like a grift for the whales with big transparent bags that paint targets on their backs. Solana’s still mostly open, privacy opt-in and clunky. Hypocrisy much?

Default privacy kills composability—private DeFi’s a ghost town, TVL pathetic. Zcash ecosystem? Barely anything real. Monero mandates privacy, owns darknets. Bitcoin has mixers, Ethereum has Railgun or Aztec.

Governance mess too—token voting’s flawed, ETFs could centralize everything.

Bigger picture: we need privacy without total anarchy. ZK proofs could let you prove taxes paid without spilling your life. Compliant privacy—view keys for auditors, incentives over bans.Zcash points there, sure. Not the villain, maybe the nudge for better rules.But this rally? Heavy on charisma, light on unbreakable demand. Competitors everywhere, adoption hurdles massive. Corrections crush the believers.

I’m not sold. Could go huge long-term, yeah. But I’ve watched too many “must-have” narratives crash on reality. Transparency sparked this whole thing; privacy might mature it—but not on hype alone.

Next time Twitter’s screaming about the new savior coin? Do this dumb thing: mute it all for a month. Then check the shielded flows cold.
