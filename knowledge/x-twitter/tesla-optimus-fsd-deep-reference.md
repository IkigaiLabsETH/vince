## 2. Full Self-Driving (FSD)

### The Promise vs. Reality

FSD is the single most contentious topic in Tesla discourse. Understanding the gap between promises and delivery is essential for reading X sentiment.

### Timeline

- **Sep 2014:** Autopilot hardware (AP1) ships on Model S. Partnership with **Mobileye** for vision processing. Basic lane-keeping and adaptive cruise control.
- **Jul 2016:** Mobileye partnership ends after fatal crash (Joshua Brown, May 2016, Model S hit a semi-truck while on Autopilot). Mobileye cited safety disagreements.
- **Oct 2016:** Musk announces all new Teslas have "Full Self-Driving hardware." Claims all cars being produced have the hardware needed for full autonomy. (They didn't â€” HW2 was insufficient, later upgraded to HW2.5, then HW3.)
- **2019:** "Full Self-Driving" package goes on sale. Initial price **$8,000**. Musk at Autonomy Day (Apr 2019): "Robotaxis by 2020. A million robotaxis on the road."
- **Oct 2020:** FSD Beta released to small group of testers. City street driving. Janky but impressive in cherry-picked videos.
- **2021-2022:** Tesla removes radar from vehicles, goes **vision-only** ("Tesla Vision"). Controversial â€” most competitors use sensor fusion (cameras + radar + lidar).
- **Sep 2022:** FSD Beta expands to ~160K users in North America.
- **2022-2023:** Price increases: $8K â†’ $12K â†’ $15K. Then Tesla adds **subscription model**: $99/mo (basic Autopilot owners) or $199/mo.
- **Dec 2023:** NHTSA forces recall of **2 million vehicles** for insufficient driver attention monitoring while Autopilot is engaged. OTA software update.
- **2024:** Rebranded from "FSD Beta" to **"FSD (Supervised)"** â€” acknowledging it requires driver supervision. Still SAE Level 2.
- **Oct 10, 2024:** "We, Robot" event. **Cybercab** unveiled â€” purpose-built robotaxi with no steering wheel or pedals. 2-seater. Also showed **Robovan** (20-passenger autonomous van). No firm production date. Stock dropped ~9% next day.
- **2025:** FSD (Supervised) v13 rollout. Improvements noted but still makes errors requiring intervention. Musk continues to say "this year" for unsupervised FSD.

### HW3 vs HW4 Schism

- **HW3** (custom Tesla chip, 2019-2023 vehicles): Musk repeatedly promised this hardware is "all you need" for full autonomy.
- **HW4** (2023+ vehicles): More cameras, better compute. Tesla has not committed to free HW4 upgrades for HW3 owners who paid $8K-$15K for FSD.
- This is a slow-burning resentment bomb on X. HW3 owners who paid for FSD feel scammed.
- Key search terms: "HW3 abandoned," "FSD refund," "HW4 upgrade"

### NHTSA & Safety

- **Multiple NHTSA investigations** into Autopilot-related crashes since 2016
- At least **40+ deaths** linked to Autopilot/FSD use (various tracking sources differ on exact count)
- **Dec 2023 recall:** 2M vehicles for Autopilot attention monitoring
- **Feb 2024 recall:** 362K vehicles for FSD Beta crash risk
- "Recall" in Tesla context = OTA software update, not physical return. But the headlines hit hard.

### The Robotaxi Question

- Waymo (Alphabet) operates actual autonomous ride-hailing in San Francisco, Phoenix, LA, Austin â€” no safety driver, paying customers.
- Cruise (GM) had similar service, paused Oct 2023 after pedestrian dragging incident, license revoked, eventually shut down.
- Tesla has **zero** autonomous ride-hailing deployments as of Feb 2026.
- Musk keeps promising "Austin robotaxi launch" â€” the date keeps shifting.
- The gap between Waymo's real service and Tesla's promises is a constant X talking point.

## 3. Optimus (Tesla Bot)

### Timeline

- **Aug 19, 2021:** Musk announces "Tesla Bot" at AI Day. A **person in a spandex bodysuit** walks on stage as the "prototype." Not a joke â€” this actually happened. Internet had a field day.
- **Sep 30, 2022:** AI Day 2022. First actual prototype ("Bumble C") shown. It could **barely walk unassisted**. A second prototype ("Optimus") was shown on a stand, could wave and move its limbs.
- **Dec 12, 2023:** Gen 2 Optimus video released. Significantly improved: walking more naturally, **dancing**, sorting objects, handling eggs without crushing them. **11 degrees of freedom (DOF)** in hands.
- **2024:** Gen 3 development. **22 DOF hands** â€” much more dexterous. Various demo videos of folding shirts, picking up objects.
- **Oct 10, 2024:** "We, Robot" event. Optimus units walk among attendees, serve drinks, interact with crowds. Impressive on camera. **But: they were largely teleoperated by humans behind the scenes.** Tesla did not disclose this. When it came out, significant criticism.
- **2025:** Limited deployment in Tesla factories for simple tasks (moving parts between stations). Musk claims thousands will be in use.
- **Jun 2025:** **Milan Kovac**, head of Optimus program, **resigns**. Replaced by **Ashok Elluswamy** (head of Autopilot/FSD). Interpreted as sign of trouble or pivot to more AI-driven approach.

### Musk's Claims

- **Price:** $20K-$30K per unit when mass-produced (for context: Boston Dynamics' Spot robot costs ~$75K and it's just a dog)
- **Timeline:** "Limited production 2025, broader production 2026"
- **Market size:** Musk has said Optimus could be worth more than all of Tesla's other businesses combined â€” potentially making Tesla a $25T company
- **Mars:** Optimus robots to be sent to Mars on SpaceX Starship. Originally 2026 timeline, later shifted to Moon colony focus (Feb 2026).
- **Home use:** Musk envisions Optimus doing household chores â€” cooking, cleaning, childcare. "Like a friend."

### Reality Check

- As of Feb 2026, Optimus cannot autonomously navigate an unstructured environment
- Factory tasks are simple pick-and-place, heavily supervised
- No unit has been sold commercially
- Teleoperation at "We, Robot" means the impressive demos were partially human-controlled
- Battery life and runtime in real-world conditions remain unproven at scale

### Expert Criticism

- **Rodney Brooks** (iRobot co-founder, MIT professor): Called humanoid robot timelines "pure fantasy." Has been tracking Musk's predictions and their failure rate on his blog.
- **Filip Piekniewski** (AI researcher): Called Optimus "complete and utter scam"
- **General robotics community:** Skeptical of Tesla's ability to leap ahead of companies that have spent decades on bipedal robotics
