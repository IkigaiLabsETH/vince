## 4. Platform Changes Under Musk

### X Premium (formerly Twitter Blue)

- Pre-Musk Twitter Blue: $4.99/mo, minor features
- **Musk's X Premium:** $8/mo (web), $11/mo (iOS/Android), up to $16/mo for Premium+
- **Verification for sale:** The blue checkmark, previously earned through identity verification of notable accounts, became a paid feature
- Legacy verified accounts lost checkmarks Apr 20 2023 unless they paid
- Immediate problems: impersonation wave. Fake "verified" accounts for Eli Lilly, Nintendo, Lockheed Martin caused real stock/PR damage
- Gold checkmarks for organizations ($1,000/mo + $50/affiliated account)

### Community Notes

- Renamed from **Birdwatch** (Twitter's pre-Musk fact-checking experiment)
- Crowdsourced fact-checking: users write context notes, rated by other contributors
- Algorithm requires agreement across users with different viewpoints to surface a note
- Generally considered one of the better changes â€” even critics credit it
- Has been applied to Musk's own tweets on occasion

### Content & Feature Expansion

- **Long-form posts:** Premium subscribers can post up to **25,000 characters** (up from 280)
- **Audio and video calls:** X added in-app calling features (Oct 2023)
- **Creator monetization:** Ad revenue sharing program launched Jul 2023. Creators get a cut of ad revenue from replies to their posts. Requires Premium subscription + 5M organic impressions in last 3 months.
- **X Hiring:** Job listing feature competing with LinkedIn
- **Spaces:** Twitter's live audio feature (pre-Musk) continued, with mixed reliability

### Grok Integration

- **Grok** (xAI's chatbot) integrated directly into the X app
- Available first to Premium+ subscribers, later expanded
- Accessible via a dedicated tab in the X app
- Can analyze trending topics, summarize threads, generate images
- Controversial: Grok has fewer content restrictions than competitors, generating political and NSFW content more freely

### Algorithm Changes

- **Mar 31 2023:** Twitter open-sources parts of its recommendation algorithm on GitHub
- Revealed: the algorithm had a specific author-level multiplier â€” **Musk's own tweets received algorithmic boosting**
- "For You" algorithmic feed became the default tab (over the chronological "Following" feed)
- Engagement metrics favor reply chains, controversy, and high-impression posts
- Paying Premium subscribers get **algorithmic boost** to their replies and posts

## 9. The OG Perspective (2007 Users)

### What Twitter Was

For people who joined Twitter in 2006-2010, the platform was something fundamentally different from what X is today.

**The chronological timeline was everything.** You saw tweets in the order they were posted. No algorithm deciding what was "relevant." No "For You" tab pushing viral content. You followed 200 people and you saw what those 200 people posted. That was it.

**Organic reach was real.** A thoughtful tweet from someone with 500 followers could spark a genuine conversation. You didn't need to game engagement metrics or post at optimal times. The playing field was flat because there was no algorithm to tilt it.

**Communities formed naturally.** Tech Twitter, Media Twitter, Black Twitter, Weird Twitter â€” these weren't hashtag campaigns or managed communities. They were emergent cultures. People found each other through replies and retweets. There was no monetization pressure because there was nothing to monetize.

**It was the actual town square.** The phrase "digital town square" gets thrown around now as marketing, but in 2008-2012, it was genuinely true. Arab Spring organizing happened on Twitter. Breaking news hit Twitter before CNN. Journalists, politicians, and regular people were in the same unfiltered stream.

### What Changed (Before Musk)

The algorithmic timeline arrived in **2016** and gradually took over. Twitter started optimizing for engagement, which meant optimizing for outrage, controversy, and volume. The shift was gradual enough that many OGs adapted without fully noticing what was lost.

### What Changed (After Musk)

The Musk era accelerated every trend that was already hollowing out the OG experience:

- **The algorithm now rewards volume and controversy over signal.** High-impression posts get boosted. Thoughtful, niche content gets buried. The "For You" tab is the default, and it's tuned for maximum engagement, not maximum quality.
- **Premium payouts incentivize engagement farming.** When creators get paid based on impressions in their reply threads, the rational move is to post inflammatory takes that generate maximum replies. This is not a side effect â€” it's the core mechanic.
- **Verification means nothing.** The blue check used to mean "this person is who they claim to be." Now it means "this person pays $8/month." For OGs who remember when verification was a trust signal, this was a gut punch.
- **Thoughtful OG voices get throttled.** If you post once a day with something considered and well-crafted, the algorithm treats you as low-engagement. The system rewards people who post 20 times a day with hot takes. Many OG users have left or gone quiet.
- **The reply guys won.** Premium subscribers' replies get boosted above everyone else's. Paying for visibility in someone's mentions would have been unthinkable in 2009.

### The Exodus

Many OG Twitter users migrated to Bluesky, Mastodon, Threads, or simply went quiet. The ones who stayed often did so out of network lock-in â€” their audience was there, their DM relationships were there, their history was there. Not because they liked what the platform had become.

The irony: Musk bought "the town square" and turned it into a mall with a cover charge.

## ECHO Context

### How This Knowledge Helps ECHO

**1. Interpreting X Sentiment Accurately**

Sentiment on X is not a neutral sample of public opinion. The platform's algorithm, monetization structure, and moderation policies all create systematic biases. ECHO needs to account for:

- **Algorithmic amplification of outrage:** Negative, controversial, and emotionally charged posts get more distribution. Raw sentiment scores from X will skew more negative/extreme than actual public sentiment.
- **Pay-to-play visibility:** Premium subscribers' posts and replies get boosted. These users skew toward Musk supporters and crypto/tech-aligned accounts. Their over-representation in visible discourse distorts topic-level sentiment.
- **Bot and engagement farming:** The monetization of impressions created an entire economy of engagement farmers. Many high-impression posts are manufactured for revenue, not genuine expression.
- **Survivorship bias:** Many moderate voices left X after the acquisition. The remaining user base is self-selected â€” people who stayed despite (or because of) the changes. This makes X sentiment more polarized than the general population.

**2. Understanding Crypto Twitter (CT) Context**

CT is one of the most active communities on X, and it was shaped by specific platform dynamics:

- Many prominent CT accounts were early adopters of X Premium because verification signaled legitimacy in a space full of scams
- CT culture is deeply intertwined with Musk's personal brand â€” his Dogecoin posts, Bitcoin comments, and general techno-optimism resonated with the community
- The shift from Twitter to X coincided with bear market â†’ recovery cycle in crypto â€” sentiment about the platform and sentiment about markets often blend together
- Musk's political arc (Trump backing â†’ DOGE advisor â†’ feud) created real splits in CT between pro-Musk and disillusioned factions

**3. Algo Bias Awareness**

When ECHO analyzes trending topics, engagement patterns, or viral posts on X, it should factor in:

- **Musk's own posts get algorithmic priority.** Any topic Musk tweets about will show inflated engagement numbers that don't reflect organic interest.
- **"For You" tab content is not representative.** It's optimized for clicks and time-on-platform, not for accurately representing what users think.
- **Community Notes are the most reliable signal.** Because they require cross-partisan agreement, noted posts with context represent a more balanced view than raw engagement metrics.
- **Reply sentiment is polluted by Premium boosting.** The most visible replies are from paying users, not necessarily the most representative voices.
