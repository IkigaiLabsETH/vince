## 1. Bitcoin (BTC)

### Fundamentals

- Created 2009 by Satoshi Nakamoto. Identity unknown. Genesis block mined Jan 3 2009.
- 21 million fixed supply cap. Hard-coded. Cannot change without consensus fork.
- Current circulating supply: ~19.8M BTC. Remaining ~1.2M mined over next ~114 years.
- Final BTC mined approximately 2140.
- Block time: ~10 minutes. Difficulty adjusts every 2,016 blocks (~2 weeks).

**Halving Cycle:**
- Block reward halves every 210,000 blocks (~4 years).
- 2012: 50â†’25 BTC. 2016: 25â†’12.5. 2020: 12.5â†’6.25. 2024: 6.25â†’3.125.
- 2024 halving: April 20 2024. Current reward: 3.125 BTC/block.
- Next halving: ~2028. Reward drops to 1.5625 BTC/block.
- Halvings historically precede major bull runs (12-18 months after). This pattern is CT gospel.

**Consensus & Mining:**
- Proof of Work. SHA-256 hashing algorithm.
- Mining is industrial scale. No garage miners anymore.
- Network hashrate: ~500 EH/s (exahashes per second). All-time highs.
- Major miners: Marathon Digital (MARA), CleanSpark (CLSK), Riot Platforms (RIOT), Core Scientific.
- Mining concentrated in US (~40%), Kazakhstan, Russia, Canada.
- Energy FUD is persistent but declining as narrative. Many miners use stranded/renewable energy.

**Layer 2 & Extensions:**
- Lightning Network: payment channels for fast, cheap BTC transactions. Growing but still niche for payments.
- Ordinals (Jan 2023): Casey Rodarmor inscribed NFTs directly on Bitcoin using witness data. Controversial. Maxis hate it ("spam"). Degens love it.
- BRC-20 tokens: fungible tokens on Bitcoin via Ordinals. Crude but functional. Created memecoin mania on BTC.
- Runes (Apr 2024): Casey Rodarmor's improved fungible token standard for Bitcoin. Launched same block as halving. Cleaner than BRC-20.
- BitVM: proposed smart contract computation on Bitcoin. Early stage.

## 2. Ethereum (ETH)

### Fundamentals

- Created by Vitalik Buterin. Whitepaper 2013. Launched July 30 2015.
- Smart contract platform. Programmable money. "World computer."
- Turing-complete VM (EVM). Solidity is the primary language.
- Current supply: ~120.2M ETH. No hard cap. But net issuance is near zero or negative post-Merge.

**The Merge (Sep 15 2022):**
- Transitioned from Proof of Work to Proof of Stake. Biggest upgrade in crypto history.
- Energy usage dropped 99.95% overnight.
- No more ETH mining. Validators stake 32 ETH to participate.
- Issuance dropped ~90%. From ~13,000 ETH/day (PoW) to ~1,700 ETH/day (PoS).

**EIP-1559 (Aug 2021 â€” London upgrade):**
- Base fee burn mechanism. Part of every transaction fee is burned (destroyed).
- When network usage is high, burn > issuance = ETH supply shrinks = deflationary.
- "Ultra sound money" meme born here. ultrasound.money tracks burn rate.
- When usage is low (bear market, L2s absorbing activity), ETH can be slightly inflationary.

**Staking:**
- ~28% of all ETH staked (~33M+ ETH).
- Staking yield: ~3-4% APR. Varies with network activity.
- Liquid staking: Lido (stETH) dominates with ~30% of staked ETH. Centralization concern.
- Other liquid staking: Rocket Pool (rETH), Coinbase (cbETH), Frax (sfrxETH).
- Restaking (EigenLayer): stake your stETH again to secure other protocols. Extra yield. Extra risk. Rehypothecation debate is real.

**Layer 2 Ecosystem:**
- ETH's scaling strategy: L1 for security/settlement, L2s for execution.
- Optimistic Rollups: Arbitrum (ARB), Optimism (OP), Base (Coinbase's L2, no token).
- ZK Rollups: zkSync (ZK), Starknet (STRK), Scroll, Linea (ConsenSys), Polygon zkEVM.
- Blast: L2 with native yield. Controversial launch (locked deposits for months).
- Base is the breakout L2 of 2024-2025. Coinbase distribution + onchain culture.
- L2 TVL combined: billions. But L2s pay very little to L1 post-EIP-4844.

**Key Upgrades:**
- EIP-4844 "Dencun" (Mar 2024): Blob transactions. L2s post data to blobs instead of calldata. Dropped L2 fees 10-100x. Great for users. Bad for ETH fee burn (less revenue to L1).
- ERC-4337: Account abstraction. Smart contract wallets. Gas sponsorship. Better UX.
- Pectra (2025): Validator improvements, increased blob capacity, further EVM enhancements.

## 3. Solana (SOL)

### Fundamentals

- Founded by Anatoly Yakovenko (ex-Qualcomm). Raj Gokal co-founder.
- Mainnet beta launched March 2020.
- High-throughput monolithic L1. Does everything on one chain. No L2 strategy.
- Theoretical TPS: ~65,000. Practical TPS: 2,000-4,000 sustained.
- Block time: 400ms. Finality: ~400ms. Feels instant.
- Transaction cost: ~$0.00025. Basically free.

**Consensus:**
- Proof of History (PoH): cryptographic clock that orders transactions before consensus.
- Combined with Tower BFT (PoS variant) and Turbine (block propagation).
- Validators: ~1,900 active. Nakamoto coefficient (decentralization measure): ~31.

**Key Infrastructure:**
- Jupiter: DEX aggregator. Largest by volume. JUP token. Jupiter team (Meow) is CT darling.

- Tensor: NFT marketplace. Dominant on Solana.
- Drift: Perpetuals DEX.
- Phantom: dominant wallet. Clean UX. "The MetaMask of Solana" but actually good.

**Firedancer:**
- Alternative validator client built by Jump Crypto from scratch in C/C++.
- Promises 1M+ TPS. Would make Solana absurdly fast.
- Testnet running. Mainnet rollout phased through 2025-2026.

**Token Extensions (Token-2022):**
- New token standard with built-in features: transfer fees, confidential transfers, interest-bearing tokens, permanent delegate, transfer hooks.

**State Compression:**
- Compressed NFTs: mint millions of NFTs for pennies using merkle trees.

## 4. HYPE (Hyperliquid)

### Fundamentals

- Hyperliquid: the largest onchain perpetuals exchange by volume.
- HYPE is the native token of the Hyperliquid L1 blockchain.
- Founded by Jeff (pseudonymous). Ex-Jane Street, Harvard math.
- Team: small, mostly ex-quant. Based nowhere specific. Very secretive.

**The L1:**
- HyperBFT consensus: derived from Hotstuff. Custom-built for orderbook performance.
- Performance: 200,000 orders per second. Sub-second finality.
- Not EVM-compatible natively. HyperEVM added later as a separate execution environment.
- Runs on dedicated hardware. Validators need beefy machines.

**Architecture â€” Two Components:**
- **HyperCore:** The native orderbook engine. Perps, spot, vaults. This is where the magic happens. Not EVM. Purpose-built for trading.
- **HyperEVM:** General-purpose EVM environment. Smart contracts, DeFi composability. Bridged to HyperCore. Allows developers to build on top of Hyperliquid's liquidity.

**No VC Funding:**
- Zero venture capital. No seed round. No Series A. No token sale.
- Team funded it themselves. Built for 2+ years before launching token.
- 100% of tokens went to community (genesis airdrop) and team allocation.
- This matters enormously for CT sentiment. "No VCs to dump on you."

**Revenue Model:**
- Trading fees (perps + spot on HyperCore).
- HIP-1 token deployment fees (dutch auctions for ticker names).
- HIP-3 deployer fees.
- Assistance Fund (AF): protocol-owned fund that buys back HYPE with revenue. Acts as a constant bid.

**Staking:**
- HYPE staked to validators for network security.
- Staking yield from inflation + fee sharing.

### The Airdrop â€” Nov 29 2024

- Largest airdrop in DeFi history by dollar value.
- 31% of total HYPE supply distributed to early users based on trading activity (points system).
- Points accumulated from Nov 2023 to Nov 2024. Heavy traders got more.

- "The airdrop that changed CT forever." Set new expectations for every future airdrop.

### HIP Framework

**HIP-1: Native Token Standard**
- Hyperliquid's equivalent of ERC-20.
- Tokens deployed on HyperCore. Traded on the native orderbook.
- Ticker names allocated via dutch auction. Popular tickers cost millions of USDC.
- Every HIP-1 token gets the same orderbook infrastructure as HYPE itself. This is the key value prop.

**HIP-2: Hyperliquidity**
- Automated market making for HIP-1 tokens.
- Protocol-provided liquidity using a bonding curve mechanism.
- Ensures every HIP-1 token has baseline liquidity from day one.
- Think of it as Uniswap-style AMM but native to the orderbook.

**HIP-3: Builder-Deployed Perpetuals**
- Allows third-party builders to deploy perpetual futures on Hyperliquid.
- This is how stocks, commodities, and indices trade onchain.
- 4 Builder DEXs currently operating:
  - **xyz:** Stocks denominated in USDC. The main equities venue.
  - **flx:** Commodities denominated in USDH. Gold, silver, copper, gas, oil.
  - **vntl:** Pre-IPO markets + indices. SPACEX, STRIPE, OPENAI. Also SPY, QQQ, IWM.
  - **km:** Traditional indices + USOIL.
- All HIP-3 perps use isolated margin (not cross-margin with core perps).
- 2x native trading fees.
- Different collateral per DEX (USDC for xyz, USDH for flx, etc.).
- Builders earn fees from their deployed markets.

## 5. HIP-4: Onchain Options & Prediction Markets (Proposed)

> **Status as of Feb 2026:** Community proposal/discussion. NOT officially published in Hyperliquid docs. No confirmed timeline.

### What HIP-4 Would Be

- Native options and prediction markets on the Hyperliquid L1.
- Would complement the existing stack: HIP-1 (spot tokens) + HIP-2 (liquidity) + HIP-3 (perps) + HIP-4 (options/predictions).
- Uses the same orderbook infrastructure that handles 200K orders/sec.

### Potential Features

- **European-style options** on any HIP-1 asset.
- **Binary options / prediction markets** â€” similar to Polymarket but onchain on Hyperliquid.
- **Same matching engine** as perps. No separate system needed.
- **Integration with Hypersurface** for automated market making (covered calls, secured puts).
- **Cash-settled** in USDC or USDH.

### Why It Matters

**Options are the last major TradFi primitive not onchain at scale:**
- Spot: done (DEXs everywhere).
- Perps: done (Hyperliquid, dYdX, GMX).
- Options: still mostly offchain. Deribit dominates crypto options. No onchain competitor at scale.
- Traditional options market is $600T+ notional. Crypto options barely scratched the surface.

**Polymarket proved prediction market demand:**
- $3.2B+ traded on Polymarket in 2024 (mostly US election).
- Demonstrated massive appetite for binary outcome betting.
- But Polymarket is a standalone app. Integrating into Hyperliquid = composability with all other HL primitives.

**Completes the Hyperliquid financial stack:**
- Spot (HIP-1) + Perps (HIP-3) + Options (HIP-4) = complete financial system.
- Add stocks/commodities via HIP-3 builders = onchain everything.
- "Why do we need Wall Street?" is the endgame narrative.

**Hypersurface connection:**
- Hypersurface already runs covered calls and secured puts on Hyperliquid.
- Native options infrastructure would make these strategies more capital efficient.
- Eliminates need for separate options infrastructure.

### Current State

- Community discussion phase. Various proposals floating around.
- No official HIP-4 document published by Hyperliquid team.
- Jeff has not publicly commented on options roadmap.
- Hypersurface operations suggest the team is thinking about it.
- Could be announced at any time. Could be years away. Unknown.

### Relevance to VINCE Stack

- Solus (VINCE's AI agent) already runs Hypersurface options strategies.
- Native HL options would eliminate need for separate infrastructure.
- Would allow more sophisticated automated trading strategies.
