# Sector Rotation Strategy

*Aligning positions with the economic cycle*

---

## The Business Cycle

### The 4 Phases

```
         Expansion
            ↑
           /  \
    Peak →     ← Trough
           \  /
            ↓
         Contraction
```

| Phase | Economic Activity | Inflation |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| Early | Rising | Low |
| Mid | Strong | Rising |
| Late | Peak | High |
| Recession | Falling | Falling |

---

## Sector Rotation Map

### Early Cycle (Recovery)

**Characteristics:**
- Fed just cut rates
- Economy bottoming
- Sentiment improving

**Best Sectors:**
| Sector | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Financials** | Net interest margin improves |
| **Consumer Discretionary** | Spending recovers |
| **Technology** | Growth re-accelerates |
| **Real Estate** | Rates falling |
| **Industrials** | CapEx returning |

**Avoid:** Utilities, Consumer Staples

---

### Mid Cycle (Expansion)

**Characteristics:**
- Growth solid
- Inflation moderate
- Fed on hold

**Best Sectors:**
| Sector | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Technology** | Earnings growth |
| **Healthcare** | Stable demand |
| **Consumer Discretionary** | Strong spending |
| **Industrials** | CapEx cycle |
| **Materials** | Demand strong |

**Avoid:** Utilities (boring)

---

### Late Cycle (Peak)

**Characteristics:**
- Economy slowing
- Inflation high
- Fed tightening

**Best Sectors:**
| Sector | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Energy** | Oil demand strong |
| **Healthcare** | Defensive |
| **Utilities** | Dividends |
| **Consumer Staples** | Non-discretionary |
| **Industrials** | Still growing |

**Avoid:** Tech, Consumer Discretionary

---

### Recession (Contraction)

**Characteristics:**
- GDP shrinking
- Unemployment rising
- Fed cutting

**Best Sectors:**
| Sector | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Utilities** | Stable cash flows |
| **Consumer Staples** | Need won't go away |
| **Healthcare** | Recession-proof |
| **Treasuries** | Safety |
| **Cash** | Wait for bottom |

**Avoid:** Cyclicals, Tech, Financials

---

## Style Rotation

### Growth vs Value

| Phase | Style | Reason |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Early | Growth | Rates fall, multiple expansion |
| Mid | Growth | Earnings growth |
| Late | Value | Safety, dividends |
| Recession | Value | Defense |

---

### Small Cap vs Large Cap

| Phase | Style | Reason |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Early | Small | More levered to recovery |
| Mid | Small | Growth |
| Late | Large | Safety |
| Recession | Large | Stability |

## The 60-Year Cycle (Kondratieff)

### Seasons of the Economy

| Season | Years | Best Sectors |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| Spring | Bottom-1/3 | Cyclicals |
| Summer | Mid | Tech, Growth |
| Autumn | Top 1/3 | Financials, Energy |
| Winter | Bottom | Staples, Utilities |

## Indicator-Based Rotation

### Leading Indicators

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Yield curve steepening | Add cyclicals |
| Yield curve flattening | Rotate to defensive |
| Spreads widening | Reduce risk |
| Volatility rising | Hedge |

---

### Momentum Indicators

| Signal | Sector |
|--------|--------|
| Relative strength ↑ | Buy sector |
| Relative strength ↓ | Sell sector |
| New high | Hold/buy |
| New low | Avoid |

## Practical Implementation

### Step-by-Step

1. **Identify cycle** (leading indicators)
2. **Overweight** leading sectors
3. **Underweight** lagging sectors
4. **Monitor** for phase changes
5. **Rotate** gradually

---
