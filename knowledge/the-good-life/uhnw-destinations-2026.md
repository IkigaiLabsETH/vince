# Top UHNW Destinations 2026 (Barnes City Index)

Curated list of top places for ultra-high-net-worth individuals (UHNWIs) to "live the life" in 2026, based on the **Barnes City Index**. The index evaluates cities on: lifestyle appeal, tax advantages, security, infrastructure, healthcare, education, culture, real estate investment potential, and overall quality of living. Rankings reflect preferences for permanent or semi-permanent residence, with emphasis on luxury, convenience, and growth opportunities. Notation in parentheses indicates rank change from 2025 (e.g. "5" = up 5 places; "-" = unchanged; "35" = up 35 places).

**Use when:** User asks where to live, relocate, invest in real estate, best cities for wealth, tax-friendly bases, or lifestyle hubs for high net worth. Focus answers on these destinations unless the user specifies others.

---

## Madrid (–)

- Affordable luxury compared to other European capitals; high-end properties appreciating steadily amid strong demand.
- Excellent work-life balance: world-class healthcare, efficient public transport, top-tier international schools, low crime.
- Vibrant culture: museums (e.g. Prado), fine dining, proximity to nature; favorable tax regimes for wealthy residents.
- Emerging tech and finance hub, international talent, sunny Mediterranean lifestyle.

## Milan (5)

- Italy’s financial centre: strong business in fashion, design, finance; solid infrastructure and connectivity.
- Tax incentives for HNWIs, including flat-rate schemes for foreign income (wealth preservation).
- Culture: opera, Pinacoteca di Brera, Quadrilatero della Moda, Lake Como and the Alps.
- High quality of life: healthcare, gourmet cuisine, sophisticated social scene; notable rank rise.

## Dubai (2)

- Tax-free personal income and capital gains; golden visa programs for investors; strong wealth growth.
- Ultra-modern infrastructure: top airports, luxury developments, safety; global business and leisure hub.
- Lavish lifestyle: beaches, Dubai Mall, fine dining, yachting, desert safaris.
- Diversification into tech, tourism, finance; strong real estate returns; cosmopolitan expat community.

## Miami (3)

- No state income tax, business-friendly; attracts tech, finance, and crypto entrepreneurs.
- Waterfront luxury, beaches, nightlife, Art Basel; year-round warm weather and outdoor life.
- Top private schools, healthcare, cultural diversity; easy access to the Caribbean.
- Booming real estate: branded residences, high appreciation; sunny alternative to traditional wealth hubs.

## Marbella (35)

- Major rank rise: premium resort destination, golden beaches, golf, exclusive marinas (e.g. Puerto Banús).
- Affordable luxury vs other European spots; strong investment potential and tax benefits for non-residents.
- Relaxed Andalusian lifestyle: Mediterranean cuisine, wellness, nature reserves; ideal for second homes or retirement.
- Safe, family-friendly: international schools, private healthcare, growing European and international expat community.

## Paris (–)

- Iconic culture: Louvre, fashion, gastronomy; sophisticated urban lifestyle.
- Strong infrastructure: high-speed rail, airports; elite education and healthcare.
- Wealth management: banking hubs, prestigious real estate (e.g. Golden Triangle).
- History and modernity: Fashion Week, nearby countryside; balanced, elegant life.

## New York (–)

- Global finance: Wall Street, venture capital, networking for high-achievers.
- Culture: Broadway, Michelin dining, world-class museums (e.g. MoMA).
- Premium real estate: Manhattan, Hamptons; high appreciation, private clubs.
- Top education (Ivy League proximity), healthcare; fast-paced, ambitious environment.

## Rome (9)

- Timeless appeal: Colosseum, art, authentic Italian cuisine; culturally immersive.
- Favorable tax regimes for retirees and investors; incentives for relocating wealth.
- Quality of life: mild climate, Villa Borghese, Mediterranean coast.
- Growing luxury real estate: renovated palazzos and villas; strong security and healthcare.

## Dallas (18)

- Business boom: low taxes, no state income tax; energy, tech, finance (Fortune 500).
- Spacious luxury estates, gated communities; privacy, golf, equestrian.
- Family-oriented: top private schools, medical districts, Dallas Arts District.
- Affordable high-end living vs coastal U.S.; warm weather, Southern hospitality.

## Budapest (14)

- Affordable European luxury: historic architecture, thermal spas, Danube riverfront; wellness-focused.
- Tax advantages for investors; residency programs; low property costs, high rental yields.
- Culture: opera, festivals, Hungarian cuisine; vineyards and countryside.
- Safe, walkable; improving infrastructure, international schools, healthcare; emerging value pick.

## London (8)

- Premier global finance: City of London; wealth management and investment.
- Luxury: West End, Michelin dining, Hyde Park, Harrods.
- Strong real estate: Mayfair; privacy, security, top education (Oxford/Cambridge nearby).
- Cosmopolitan; multicultural; major airports and connectivity.

## Athens (16)

- History: Acropolis, ancient ruins; island-hopping and Mediterranean yachting.
- Golden visa for property investors; EU residency and tax perks.
- Revitalized culture: museums, festivals, seafood; improving urban infrastructure.
- Affordable luxury: Athens Riviera, private beaches, healthcare, family-friendly.

## Abu Dhabi (11)

- Tax-free; sovereign wealth; business incentives in oil, finance, renewables.
- Opulent lifestyle: Louvre Abu Dhabi, beaches, Saadiyat Island.
- High security, healthcare, international schools; family-oriented communities.
- Strong real estate growth; waterfront developments; proximity to Dubai.

## Monaco (4)

- Ultimate tax haven: no income tax; privacy and asset protection.
- Exclusive lifestyle: yacht shows, Formula 1, casinos, Michelin dining.
- Compact, secure; top healthcare, international education, Mediterranean views.
- High-value real estate; scarcity drives appreciation; slight rank drop vs emerging hubs.

## Mallorca (new entrant)

- Serene island: pristine beaches, mountains, luxury villas; relaxed, nature-focused living.
- Favorable Spanish tax rules for non-residents; EU access; strong property investment.
- Wellness: hiking, sailing, golf; farm-to-table, local vineyards and markets.
- Safe, family-friendly; international schools, private healthcare; easy flights to mainland; second-home paradise.
