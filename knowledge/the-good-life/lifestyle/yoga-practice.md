# Yoga Practice Guide

Our yoga practice is integral to the Okerson Protocol philosophy: **movement as medicine, recovery as foundation**.

## Member-Specific Approaches

### Katarina - Daily Vinyasa Practice

**Primary Resource:** [Yoga with Kassandra](https://www.youtube.com/@yogawithkassandra)

**Philosophy:**
- Daily practice for consistency and mental clarity
- Vinyasa flow for breath-movement connection
- Mix of energizing morning flows and calming evening sessions

**Recommended Session Types:**
- **Morning Energy:** 20-30 min vinyasa flow to start the day
- **Midday Reset:** 15-20 min gentle stretch
- **Evening Wind-Down:** 20-30 min yin or restorative

**Kassandra Favorites:**
- Morning yoga flows (15-30 min)
- Full body vinyasa sequences
- Yin yoga for deep stretching
- Yoga for stress relief and relaxation

### Yves - Surfer Yoga & 50+ Mobility

**Focus Areas (aligned with Okerson Protocol):**
- Hip openers (critical for paddling and pop-ups)
- Shoulder mobility (paddling recovery)
- Spine rotation (turning on the board)
- Balance work (proprioception)
- Joint protection (50+ considerations)

**Surfer-Specific Sequences:**

1. **Pre-Surf Warm-Up (10-15 min)**
   - Cat-cow for spine mobility
   - Thread the needle for thoracic rotation
   - Hip circles and lunges
   - Shoulder rolls and arm circles
   - Balance poses (tree, eagle)

2. **Post-Surf Recovery (20-30 min)**
   - Deep hip openers (pigeon, lizard)
   - Shoulder stretches (eagle arms, cow face arms)
   - Spine decompression (child's pose, forward folds)
   - Gentle twists for spine release
   - Savasana for nervous system reset

3. **50+ Mobility Focus (15-20 min)**
   - Joint-friendly movements
   - Longer holds (30-60 seconds)
   - Props encouraged (blocks, straps)
   - Emphasis on breath over depth
   - Avoid extreme ranges of motion

**Recommended YouTube Channels for Surfers:**
- Yoga for Surfers sequences
- Mobility-focused yoga for athletes
- Yin yoga for deep tissue release

## Integration with Okerson Protocol

### Sunday Active Recovery
The Okerson Protocol specifically recommends yoga on Sundays:
> "Active recovery: yoga or long barefoot walk"

**Sunday Yoga Session:**
- 30-45 min restorative or yin
- Focus on recovery from week's training
- Sauna + cold contrast afterward
- No phone, meditative quality

### Movement as Medicine
Core principle: yoga supports the body's natural healing capacity
- Low-impact, high-benefit
- Enhances sleep quality
- Reduces cortisol
- Improves range of motion for all activities

### Recovery Stack Integration
Yoga pairs with:
- Post-swim sessions (hip focus from frog kick)
- Morning sunlight practice (outdoor when possible)
- Evening wind-down before 9pm screens-off

## Philosophy Notes

From looksmaxxing.md:
> "Movement, sunlight, cold water... Win the morning, win the day."

Yoga is not separate from the training protocol - it's the connective tissue that makes everything else sustainable. The 50+ body needs daily mobility work to maintain the capacity for hill sprints, heavy carries, and swimming.

**Remember:**
- Consistency over intensity
- Recovery enables performance
- Movement is medicine
- The best practice is the one you'll do every day
