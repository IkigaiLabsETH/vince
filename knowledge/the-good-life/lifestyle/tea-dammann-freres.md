## Morning tea (by profile)

From Dammann's "Best morning teas" editorial. Suggest by how they wake up or what they want.

**1. Wake up easily** — Subtle, mature flavour. Plain Darjeeling or Ceylon: **Dammann Phuguri**, **Darjeeling Jungpana 2nd Flush F.T.G.F.O.P.**, **Superior Darjeeling G.F.O.P.**, **Kenilworth Ceylon O.P.**

**2. Pick-me-up / energy** — Sharper, more kick at breakfast. **Strong Breakfast**, **Royal Breakfast**, **Mélange des Seigneurs T.G.F.O.P.**

**3. With milk** — Assam or full-bodied teas; milk enhances them. Can steep tea directly in milk if they like. **Assam Halmari 2nd flush T.G.B.O.P.**, **Superior Assam G.B.O.P.**, **Africain Breakfast**.

**4. In a rush** — One cup, filling, no caffeine jitters. Yunnan "Mochas of tea": **Grand Yunnan G.F.O.P.**, **Superior Grand Yunnan G.F.O.P.**

**5. Green tea lovers** — **Green Fujian**, **Printemps sur le lac**, **Genmaïcha**.

## Evening tea (caffeine-free)

For relaxation, after dinner, winding down. Only suggest caffeine-free options in the evening.

**Herbal infusions:** Chamomile, Verbena, Peppermint, **Tisane fleur d'oranger** (orange blossom, chamomile), **Tisane Fidji** (lemon, lemongrass, ginger).

**Rooibos:** Naturally caffeine-free; honeyed, smooth. **Rooibos Earl Grey** (bergamot + rooibos—"another way to savour a classic until quite late in the day"). **Rooibos Vanille** (vanilla, gourmet, full-bodied). **Rooibos Oriental** (passion fruit, peach, wild strawberry).

**Fruit infusions:** Caffeine-free; fruity and light.

**Preparation:** Herbal and rooibos — 5–6 min in boiling water (dried plants need longer infusion).
