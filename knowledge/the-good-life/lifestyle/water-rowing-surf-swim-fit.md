# Water Rowing: Surf and Swim Fit

## Your human's setup

- **Equipment:** Water rowing machine at home.
- **Goal:** Use it **way more** than currently; get **surf fit** and **swim fit**.
- **Context:** Primary sport is 1000m+ daily swimming (see swimming-daily-winter-pools); pool heating off early Dec–end Feb (gym season). Rowing is an ideal indoor option in gym season or as extra cardio in pool season.

---

## Why it transfers to surf and swim

- **Cardio base:** Builds aerobic endurance that directly supports 1000m+ swims and long paddling sessions. Same engine; rower trains it without pool or ocean.
- **Power chain:** Leg drive → core → back/shoulders mirrors the pull phase in swimming and paddling on a board. Strengthens the same chain—lats, rhomboids, core, leg drive—in one movement.
- **Rhythm and breathing:** Continuous stroke and breath timing are similar to lap swimming. Good for pacing and mental carryover when they get back in the pool or on the board.
- **Low impact:** Sustainable alongside daily swim when they want a second session, or when pool is unavailable (gym season, travel).

---

## How to get the most out of it

### Technique

- **Drive:** Legs → body swing → arms. One smooth connection; no jerking. Power from the legs first.
- **Recovery:** Arms → body → legs. Slower than the drive so the flywheel doesn't run away.
- **Ratio:** Aim for about 1:2 (e.g. 1 count drive, 2 count recovery). Keeps stroke quality and prevents rushing.
- **Breath:** Exhale on the drive, inhale on the recovery—same idea as swimming.

### Session types

- **Steady state (20–30 min):** Rate 18–22 spm; conversational pace. Builds base; do most sessions like this.
- **Intervals:** e.g. 4×5 min with 2 min rest (rate 22–26 on work intervals); or 1 min hard / 1 min easy for 20 min. Adds intensity without burning out.
- **Longer continuous:** 30–45 min at steady state when they have time; good for weekend or recovery day.
- **Benchmark:** Occasional 10k or 20 min time trial to track progress; not every week.

### Frequency

- **Aim for 2–4x per week** to "use it way more." In gym season (Dec–Feb), rower can be primary indoor cardio when pool is cold or palace pools are closed. In pool season, add as a second session or swap on days they can't get to the pool.

### Progression

- Build consistent volume first (e.g. 20 min 3x/week), then add one interval session or one longer row per week. Avoid going hard every day; most sessions should be steady and sustainable.

## Integration with swim and yoga

- **Swimmer yoga:** Same as for pool. Pre-row: chest/thoracic/shoulders (see yoga-vinyasa-surfers-swimmers). Post-row: lats, pecs, hip flexors, thoracic twist—rowing uses the same pull chain as swimming, so the same recovery sequence works.
- **Pool season vs gym season:** In gym season, rower is an ideal indoor option when pool heating is off or palace pools are closed. In pool season, rower can be added as extra cardio or on days they skip the pool.
