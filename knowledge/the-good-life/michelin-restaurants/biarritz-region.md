# Michelin Restaurants: Biarritz & Basque Country

## Overview

The Basque Country straddles France and Spain, creating a unique gastronomic culture. The French side offers Atlantic seafood, Basque traditions, and proximity to San Sebastián—one of the world's highest concentrations of Michelin stars per capita.

## 3-Star Restaurants

### Les Prés d'Eugénie (Eugénie-les-Bains)
- **Chef**: Michel Guérard
- **Distance**: 1 hour from Biarritz
- **Style**: Pioneer of cuisine minceur and thermal cuisine
- **Experience**: Complete hotel-restaurant-spa destination
- **Notes**: Living legend, invented lighter French cooking
- **Must Order**: Tasting menu, seasonal specialties

## 2-Star Restaurants

### L'Auberge Basque (Saint-Pée-sur-Nivelle)
- **Chef**: Cédric Béchade
- **Distance**: 15 min from Biarritz
- **Style**: Contemporary Basque with roots
- **Setting**: Rural inn atmosphere
- **Notes**: Best local Basque starred option

### Maison Bras (Laguiole - Aveyron)
- **Distance**: 3 hours (roadtrip destination)
- **Style**: Terroir-driven, wild ingredients
- **Notes**: Worth special journey through countryside

## Pintxos Culture (San Sebastián)

Not Michelin-starred, but essential:

### Old Town Bars
- **Gandarias** - Classic, ham focus
- **Borda Berri** - Creative pintxos
- **La Cuchara de San Telmo** - Hot pintxos
- **Txepetxa** - Anchovy specialists
- **Bar Nestor** - Famous tomato salad, steak

### Route Strategy
- Start 19:00-20:00 (bars open)
- 2-3 pintxos per bar maximum
- Move between 4-6 bars
- End with dinner or continue grazing

## Basque Specialties

### Must Experience
- **Pintxos**: Basque tapas tradition
- **Txakoli**: Slightly fizzy local white wine
- **Chipirones**: Baby squid, grilled or in ink
- **Merluza**: Hake, prepared various ways
- **Bacalao**: Salt cod (pil-pil, vizcaína)
- **Jamón de Bayonne**: Local cured ham
- **Gâteau Basque**: Cherry or cream filled cake
- **Espelette Pepper**: Local spice

### Wine & Cider
- Irouléguy (Basque French wines)
- Txakoli (Basque Spanish whites)
- Basque cider houses (sagardotegi)

## Planning Gastronomic Trips

### Biarritz Base (3 days)
- Day 1: Arrive, L'Impertinent dinner
- Day 2: San Sebastián pintxos crawl, Akelarre dinner
- Day 3: Briketenia lunch, beach afternoon

### Les Prés d'Eugénie Pilgrimage
- 2-night minimum stay recommended
- Full spa + gastronomy experience
- Day trip to Basque coast possible

### Cross-Border Route
- Biarritz → Hondarribia → San Sebastián
- Multiple star options along route
- Combine beach + gastronomy

## Booking Strategy

### San Sebastián 3-Stars
- Book 2-3 months minimum
- Easier for lunch
- Consider weekdays

### Local French Stars
- 2-4 weeks typically fine
- Summer busier (tourists)
- September excellent (locals return)

### Pintxos
- No reservation needed
- Thursday-Saturday best atmosphere
- Arrive early for best selection
