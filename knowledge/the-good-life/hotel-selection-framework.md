# Hotel Selection Framework

## Context

Hotel selection is often done poorly - people optimize for price, or stars, or location, without considering the full picture. The right framework considers:
1. What you're trying to accomplish
2. What the hotel excels at
3. How to time it for maximum value

This is especially true for luxury hotels where the difference between a mediocre and exceptional experience can be enormous despite similar pricing.

### Step 3: Optimize Timing

**Off-Season Strategy (Our Preference):**
```
Peak pricing: ~800-1200 EUR/night
Off-season pricing: ~400-600 EUR/night
Savings: 40-50%

Plus: Quieter, better service, available reservations
Minus: Some facilities may be closed, weather less reliable
```

**Day of Week Optimization:**
```
Best rates: Wednesday, Thursday, Sunday
Worst rates: Friday, Saturday (unless business hotel)
```

**1-Night Stay Maximization:**
```

19:30 - Pre-dinner drinks

12:00 - Late checkout, explore grounds
13:00 - Depart

Total experience time: 24 hours
Value extracted: Lunch, spa, dinner, breakfast, sleep, grounds
```

## Application: Hotel Category Selection

### For Gastronomy Trips

**Prioritize (in order):**
1. On-site starred restaurant (3* > 2* > 1*)
2. Quality of wine program
3. Multiple dining options (formal + casual)
4. Kitchen garden or local sourcing philosophy

**De-prioritize:**
- Pool/beach (you're here to eat)
- Gym (unnecessary)
- Room size (you'll be in restaurant)

### For Relaxation/Wellness Trips

**Prioritize (in order):**
1. Spa quality and treatment options
2. Setting and views
3. Quiet/seclusion
4. Comfortable bed and room

**De-prioritize:**
- Restaurant stars (good enough is fine)
- Location convenience (you're staying put)
- Activities/excursions (you're resting)

### For Special Occasions

**Prioritize (in order):**
1. Wow factor / Instagram moments
2. Exceptional service
3. Room upgrade potential
4. Celebration-friendly (cake, champagne, etc.)

**De-prioritize:**
- Value optimization (it's a splurge)
- Practicality (it's about the experience)

## Signals and Patterns

**Quality signals:**
- Palace designation (France) or equivalent recognition
- Relais & ChÃ¢teaux membership (food focus)
- Michelin-starred restaurant on-site
- Small number of rooms (attention to detail)
- Family-owned for multiple generations

**Value signals:**
- Direct booking often includes extras
- Off-season rates 40-50% below peak
- Midweek rates 20-30% below weekend
- Package deals (dinner included)

**Red flag signals:**
- Endless renovations
- Management changes
- Michelin demotion
- Poor reviews mentioning attitude (not just issues)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Optimizing only for price**
   - Fix: Calculate experience-per-euro, not room-per-euro

2. **Ignoring timing**
   - Fix: Same hotel, 50% less in off-season

3. **Booking too many nights**
   - Fix: 1-2 nights at exceptional > 5 nights at mediocre

4. **Skipping hotel restaurant**
   - Fix: The restaurant often IS the reason to stay

5. **Arriving late, leaving early**
   - Fix: Arrive for lunch, leave after late checkout

6. **Not booking direct**
   - Fix: Luxury hotels often match OTAs + give extras

## Conclusion

- Hotel selection is goal-first, not price-first
- Match hotel strengths to your specific purpose
- Off-season timing is the biggest value lever
- 1-night stays should be experience-dense
- Quality signals matter more than star counts
