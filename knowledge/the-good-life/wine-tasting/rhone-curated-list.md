## Les vins blancs de la Vallée du Rhône (White Wines of the Rhône Valley)

### Côtes du Rhône (white)

- **Château des Tours 2011** — Côtes du Rhône (Rayas/Emmanuel Reynaud stable); red and white produced. White: Roussanne/Viognier-led, rich, floral; drink now. 10–12 °C.
- **Fonsalette 2000** — Château de Fonsalette (Côtes du Rhône; same family as Rayas). Red and white; rare, traditional. 2000 is mature; white: waxy, stone fruit if still sound. 10–12 °C.

### Condrieu (white — Viognier)

- **Domaine Vernay — Côteau de Vernon 2012** — Condrieu from one of the great sites; Domaine Georges Vernay is a reference. Côteau de Vernon: floral, apricot, fine; classic Condrieu. 10–12 °C; lobster, scallops, Asian-inspired. Drink within 5–8 years of vintage.

### Châteauneuf-du-Pape (white)

- **Château Beaucastel 2012** — Beaucastel makes serious white Châteauneuf (Roussanne-led); rich, nutty, age-worthy. 10–12 °C; poultry, lobster, cream sauces.
- **Château La Nerthe 2011** — La Nerthe white Châteauneuf; Roussanne/Grenache blanc; full, stone fruit. 10–12 °C.
- **Château Rayas 1999** — Rayas is legendary for red; white Châteauneuf is rare and traditional. 1999 is mature; check condition; 10–12 °C.
- **Château Vieux Télégraphe 1999** — Vieux Télégraphe white (La Crau); Roussanne-led; 1999 mature. 10–12 °C; drink now.

## Les vins rouges de la Vallée du Rhône (Red Wines of the Rhône Valley)

### Côtes du Rhône (red)

- **Château des Tours 2010** — Côtes du Rhône (Rayas family); Grenache-led, traditional, elegant. Good value for the stable. 16–18 °C; decant 30 min.
- **Château des Tours 2011** — Same estate; 2011 drinking now. 16–18 °C.
- **Château des Tours — Vin de Pays de Vaucluse 2011** — Table wine from the same estate; earlier-drinking, fruity. 16–17 °C.
- **Château de Fonsalette 2000** — Fonsalette Côtes du Rhône (Rayas/ Reynaud); Grenache, Cinsault, Syrah. Rare, traditional, age-worthy. 2000 mature; decant briefly; 16–18 °C.

### Saint-Joseph (red — Syrah)

- **Domaine François Villard — Reflet 2011** — Saint-Joseph; Villard is a reference in Northern Rhône. Reflet: red fruit, pepper, elegant. 16–18 °C; lamb, charcuterie.
- **Domaine François Villard — Mairlant 2011** — Saint-Joseph lieu-dit or cuvée; same estate. 16–18 °C.
- **Domaine Vernay — La Dame Brune 2012** — Saint-Joseph from Vernay (Condrieu estate); La Dame Brune is a named cuvée. Syrah; red fruit, spice. 16–18 °C.

### Hermitage (red — Syrah)

- **Maison Paul Jaboulet Aîné — « La Chapelle » 2004** — Jaboulet Hermitage La Chapelle; one of the most famous Hermitage cuvées. 2004 in a good drinking window; powerful, dark fruit, spice, long. Decant 45–60 min; 16–18 °C; lamb, game.

### Crozes-Hermitage (red — Syrah)

- **Domaine Alain Graillot 2013** — Crozes-Hermitage; Graillot is a benchmark for value and quality in Crozes. 2013 drinking now; red fruit, pepper, fresh. 16–17 °C; grilled meat, everyday red. See also crozes-hermitage.md.

### Châteauneuf-du-Pape (red)

- **Henri Bonneau 2004** — Henri Bonneau (Réserve des Célestins or Cuvée Marie Beurrier tier); legendary traditional producer. 2004 powerful, Grenache-led; decant 45–60 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Château La Nerthe 2009** — La Nerthe Châteauneuf rouge; classic blend, ripe, garrigue. 2009 drinking now; 16–18 °C.
- **Château La Nerthe — Cuvée des Cadettes 2005** — Top cuvée from La Nerthe; old vines, limited production. 2005 structured, age-worthy; decant 45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Château Beaucastel — Hommage à Jacques Perrin 2012** — Beaucastel’s top red; Mourvèdre-dominant, age-worthy. 90–98+ in top vintages. Decant 1 hour; 16–18 °C; landmark wine.
- **Château Rayas 2000** — Rayas Châteauneuf (100% Grenache from sandy soils); silky, floral, rare. 2000 drinking now; decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Château Rayas 2001** — Same estate; 2001 excellent vintage; decant 30–45 min.
- **Château Rayas 2004** — Same estate; 2004 in a good window; decant 30–45 min.
- **Château Vieux Télégraphe 2001** — Vieux Télégraphe (La Crau); Grenache-led, classic. 2001 drinking now; decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.

## When to use this list

- **Rhône white / Condrieu / Châteauneuf white:** Use the "Vins blancs" section; lead with Vernay Condrieu, Beaucastel/La Nerthe/Rayas/VT white Châteauneuf, or Château des Tours/Fonsalette white.
- **Rhône red / Hermitage / Crozes / Saint-Joseph / Châteauneuf:** Use the "Vins rouges" section; lead with Jaboulet La Chapelle, Graillot Crozes, Villard/Vernay Saint-Joseph, or Châteauneuf (Rayas, Beaucastel Hommage, La Nerthe Cadettes, Henri Bonneau, Vieux Télégraphe).
- **Specific bottle from this list:** Use the entry above; for exact drinking window or score, use WEB_SEARCH if needed.

## Service (summary)

- **Whites (Condrieu, Châteauneuf blanc):** 10–12 °C. Serve Condrieu relatively soon after opening to keep aromatics.
- **Reds:** 16–18 °C. Decant young Hermitage, Châteauneuf, and structured Côtes du Rhône 30–60 min; older bottles briefly or not at all.
