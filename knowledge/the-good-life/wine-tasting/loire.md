# Loire Valley, France

## Overview

Diverse region: crisp Sauvignon Blanc (Sancerre, Pouilly-Fumé), Chenin Blanc (Vouvray, Savennières), Melon de Bourgogne (Muscadet), and Cabernet Franc (Chinon, Bourgueil). Styles range from bone-dry to sweet and sparkling. Generally food-friendly, often good value.

## Whites

- **Sancerre & Pouilly-Fumé:** Sauvignon Blanc. Sancerre = flinty, citrus, gooseberry; Pouilly-Fumé = similar, sometimes slightly smokier ("fumé"). Both: oysters, goat cheese (e.g. Crottin de Chavignol), salads, light seafood. **When to recommend:** "Crisp white," "Sauvignon," seafood, lunch, apéritif.
- **Vouvray:** Chenin Blanc. Can be dry (sec), off-dry (demi-sec), or sweet (moelleux); also sparkling. Dry = apple, honey, mineral; sweet = apricot, honey, long-lived. Pair dry with fish and poultry; sweet with fruit desserts, blue cheese. **When to recommend:** "Something different from Sauvignon," "Chenin," versatile white.
- **Savennières:** Chenin Blanc; dry, intense, mineral, age-worthy. Serious white; shellfish, roast fish.
- **Muscadet (Sèvre et Maine):** Melon de Bourgogne; light, saline, often "sur lie" (lees-aged). Classic with oysters and mussels. **When to recommend:** Oysters, shellfish, light lunch.

## Reds

- **Chinon & Bourgueil:** Cabernet Franc. Red fruit, pencil lead, herbs; can be light and chillable or more structured. **Chinon** often a bit softer; **Bourgueil** can be firmer. Pair with lamb, duck, grilled meat, vegetarian dishes with herbs. **When to recommend:** "Light red," "Cabernet Franc," "something for lunch" (chilled), roast lamb.
- **Saumur-Champigny:** Cabernet Franc; similar profile; good value.

## Sparkling

- **Crémant de Loire:** Traditional method; Chenin, Cabernet, Chardonnay. Good value alternative to Champagne for apéritif or celebration.

## Summary

- **Crisp white, oysters, goat cheese:** Sancerre or Pouilly-Fumé.
- **Versatile white, different:** Vouvray (dry or demi-sec).
- **Shellfish, light:** Muscadet.
- **Light red, chillable:** Chinon or Bourgueil.
- **Celebration, value bubbles:** Crémant de Loire.

## Producers to know

- **Sancerre / Pouilly-Fumé:** Pascal Cotat, François Cotat, Didier Dagueneau (Pouilly-Fumé), Alphonse Mellot, Vatan. Goat cheese and oysters: Cotat, Dagueneau.
- **Vouvray:** Domaine Huet (sec, demi-sec, moelleux; age-worthy), François Chidaine, Marc Brédif.
- **Muscadet:** Domaine de la Pépière, Luneau-Papin, Marc Ollivier (sur lie).
- **Chinon / Bourgueil:** Bernard Baudry, Olga Raffault, Domaine de la Chevalerie (Bourgueil), Charles Joguet.
- **Crémant de Loire:** Gratien & Meyer, Langlois-Château.

## Vintage (short)

Sancerre and Muscadet: drink young (1–4 years) for freshness. Vouvray sec and demi-sec: 5–15 years for top bottles (e.g. Huet). Chinon/Bourgueil: 3–10 years; some age longer. Recent strong years: 2019, 2020 for whites and reds.

## Service

- **White (Sancerre, Muscadet, Vouvray sec):** 8–10 °C. Do not overchill.
- **Red (Chinon, Bourgueil):** 12–14 °C; can serve lightly chilled in summer. Decant structured examples 15–30 min if young.

## Guest-led

- If they want **mineral, no oak** → Sancerre or Muscadet (Pépière, Luneau-Papin); oysters or goat cheese.
- If they want **value bubbles** → Crémant de Loire (Gratien & Meyer).
- If they want **light red, chillable** → Chinon (Baudry, Raffault) or Bourgueil; "serve slightly chilled with lunch."
