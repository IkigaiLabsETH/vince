# White Burgundy Deep Dive — Meursault, Puligny, Chassagne & Beyond

For Chablis, see `chablis.md`. This file covers the Côte de Beaune and Mâconnais — where Chardonnay reaches its peak expression.

## The Hierarchy (What the Label Tells You)

1. **Grand Cru** — Top of the pyramid. Named vineyard on label, no village name needed. Examples: Montrachet, Bâtard-Montrachet, Chevalier-Montrachet, Corton-Charlemagne. Prices: €200-5,000+.
2. **Premier Cru (1er Cru)** — Village name + vineyard name. Examples: Meursault Perrières, Puligny-Montrachet Les Pucelles. Prices: €50-500.
3. **Village** — Village name only (e.g., "Meursault," "Puligny-Montrachet"). Prices: €25-100.
4. **Bourgogne Blanc** — Regional. Simple but can be excellent from top domaines. Prices: €12-30.

**The secret:** A village-level Meursault from a top domaine (Roulot, Lafon) will outperform a premier cru from a mediocre producer. Always buy the producer first, the vineyard second.

## MEURSAULT

### Character

Rich, nutty, buttery (when well-made, not flabby). Hazelnut, citrus, stone fruit, subtle oak. The most generous and approachable of the great white Burgundy villages. Less taut than Puligny, more opulent than Chassagne.

### The Premier Crus to Know

- **Perrières** — The greatest Meursault. Stony, mineral, powerful — almost Grand Cru quality. The "honorary Grand Cru." Taut, long, age-worthy (10-20 years). THE bottle to buy.
- **Charmes** — Softer, rounder, more immediately appealing. Richer, less mineral than Perrières. Drink 5-12 years.
- **Genevrières** — Between Perrières and Charmes in style. Floral, mineral, elegant. Excellent.
- **Goutte d'Or** — Rich, golden, opulent. Less refined but generous.
- **Poruzots** — Mineral, tense, underrated. Good value among Meursault 1er Cru.
- **Blagny** — Higher altitude, leaner, more linear. Can be outstanding for those who like tension.

### The Producers (Who to Buy)

- **Domaine des Comtes Lafon** — Dominique Lafon. THE reference for Meursault. Perrières, Charmes, Genevrières, Clos de la Barre. Biodynamic. 95-99 JD. Perrières 2019 = 96 JD. ~€150-300 for 1er Cru. Allocations.
- **Domaine Roulot** — Jean-Marc Roulot (also an actor!). Perrières, Charmes, Luchets, Tessons. Precise, mineral, less oaky than Lafon. 94-97. 1er Cru ~€120-250. Village ~€60-80. Extremely allocated.
- **Coche-Dury** — Jean-François Coche. LEGEND. Tiny production. Meursault, Perrières, and the mythical Corton-Charlemagne. 97-100. Prices: €300-3,000+ depending on cuvée. If you find it, buy it. Don't think.
- **Arnaud Ente** — Mineral, precise, modern. Small production. 93-96. Village Meursault ~€80-120.
- **Patrick Javillier** — Reliable, well-made. Cuvée des Forgets, Clos du Cromin. ~€40-60 village. Good value.
- **Antoine Jobard** — Genevrières, Poruzots, Blagny. Structured, age-worthy, less flashy. 93-96. Great value.
- **Rémi Jobard** — Cousin of Antoine. Genevrières, Charmes. Precise, mineral. ~€50-80 for 1er Cru.
- **Vincent Dancer** — Rising star. Small, precise. Perrières = stunning. 94-96.
- **Pierre-Yves Colin-Morey** — Ex-Marc Colin (Chassagne). Now in Meursault. Excellent across the board. 93-96.
- **Négociants:** Louis Jadot (Perrières, Genevrières — 92-95), Bouchard Père (reliable), Olivier Leflaive (Puligny-based but good Meursault).

### Meursault Price Guide

- Village (good producer): €40-80
- Premier Cru (Charmes, Genevrières): €80-200
- Perrières (top producer): €150-400
- Coche-Dury anything: €300-3,000+

## PULIGNY-MONTRACHET

### Character

The most refined, mineral, and taut of the great white Burgundy villages. Citrus, white flowers, wet stone, hazelnut. Less immediately generous than Meursault — more intellectual, more vertical. Ages magnificently.

### The Grand Crus (shared with Chassagne)

- **Montrachet** — THE greatest white wine vineyard on earth. Shared between Puligny and Chassagne. Producers: DRC (tiny, ~€5,000+), Domaine Leflaive (~€2,000+), Bouchard Père, Ramonet, Comtes Lafon. Power, complexity, mineral, infinite length. 97-100.
- **Chevalier-Montrachet** — Uphill from Montrachet. Higher altitude, more mineral, less rich. Some argue it's more elegant than Montrachet. Leflaive, Jadot, Bouchard. 96-99. ~€300-1,000.
- **Bâtard-Montrachet** — Below Montrachet. Richer, more opulent, broader. Leflaive, Sauzet, Ramonet. 95-98. ~€200-800.
- **Bienvenues-Bâtard-Montrachet** — Small, adjacent to Bâtard. Similar but slightly lighter. Leflaive, Sauzet. 94-97. ~€200-500.
- **Criots-Bâtard-Montrachet** — Entirely in Chassagne. Tiny. Rich. Rare.

### The Premier Crus to Know

- **Les Pucelles** — The most famous Puligny 1er Cru. Mineral, floral, precise. Just below Grand Cru level. Leflaive = reference (95-97). ~€150-400.
- **Les Folatières** — Rich, generous, more sun-exposed. Excellent. Jadot, Leflaive, Chartron. ~€80-200.
- **Clavoillon** — Leflaive monopole. Mineral, taut. ~€120-250.
- **Les Combettes** — Rich, opulent, bordering Meursault. 93-96. ~€80-200.
- **Champ Canet** — Mineral, structured. Good value among Puligny 1er Cru.
- **Les Referts** — Ripe, generous. ~€70-150.
- **Clos de la Garenne** — Small, precise. Duc de Magenta (Jadot). ~€80-120.

### The Producers

- **Domaine Leflaive** — THE reference for Puligny. Biodynamic. Brice de la Morandière (since Anne-Claude's passing). Pucelles, Clavoillon, Folatières, and the Grand Crus. 95-99. Village ~€80-120. 1er Cru ~€150-400. Grand Cru ~€500-2,000+. Allocated.
- **Domaine Étienne Sauzet** — Pucelles, Combettes, Champ Canet, Bâtard. Rich, precise. 93-97. 1er Cru ~€80-200.
- **Jean-Marc Boillot** — Combettes, Champ Canet, Referts. Generous, sometimes oaky. ~€60-150.
- **Domaine Paul Pernot** — Bienvenues-Bâtard, Pucelles, Folatières. Excellent, underpriced. 93-96. One of Puligny's best values.
- **Domaine Jacques Prieur** — Montrachet, Chevalier-Montrachet. Historic estate. 94-97.
- **Chartron et Trébuchet / Jean Chartron** — Chevalier-Montrachet Clos des Chevaliers (monopole). 94-96. ~€200-400.
- **Olivier Leflaive** — Négociant arm. More accessible, lower prices. Village ~€35-50. 1er Cru ~€60-120. Reliable.

### Puligny Price Guide

- Village (good producer): €50-120
- Premier Cru: €80-400
- Grand Cru (Bâtard, Bienvenues): €200-800
- Chevalier-Montrachet: €300-1,000
- Montrachet: €1,000-5,000+

## CHASSAGNE-MONTRACHET

### Character

More earthy, broader, and slightly less mineral than Puligny. Stone fruit, hazelnut, honey, sometimes a hint of reduction. Can be powerful. Often underestimated — some argue best value among the Big Three.

### Premier Crus to Know

- **Les Grandes Ruchottes** — Mineral, taut, high on the slope. Jadot = reference.
- **Morgeot** — Large, variable; best parcels are rich and structured. Multiple producers.
- **Les Caillerets** — Elegant, mineral. Close to Puligny border.
- **En Remilly** — Fine, precise. Guy Amiot.
- **Les Vergers** — Rich, generous.

### Producers

- **Domaine Ramonet** — The Chassagne legend. Montrachet, Bâtard, Bienvenues, Ruchottes, Morgeot. 94-98. Village ~€50-80. 1er Cru ~€80-200.
- **Domaine Marc Colin / Pierre-Yves Colin-Morey** — Excellent, precise. En Remilly, Caillerets. 93-96.
- **Michel Niellon** — Small, exceptional. Chevalier-Montrachet, Bâtard. 94-97. Hard to find.
- **Jean-Noël Gagnard / Caroline Lestimé** — Les Caillerets, Morgeot. 93-96. Good value.
- **Guy Amiot** — En Remilly, Vergers. Reliable, well-priced. ~€40-80 for 1er Cru.
- **Négociants:** Louis Jadot (Grandes Ruchottes — 93-95), Joseph Drouhin (Marquis de Laguiche Montrachet — 96-98), Bouchard Père.

### Chassagne Price Guide

- Village: €30-60 (BEST VALUE among the Big Three)
- Premier Cru: €50-200
- Grand Cru: €200-800+

## CORTON-CHARLEMAGNE

### Character

Grand Cru on the hill of Corton (Côte de Beaune). The only Grand Cru white outside the Montrachet enclave. Powerful, mineral, austere in youth — needs time. Citrus, smoke, flint, white flowers. 10-20+ year aging potential. Sometimes compared to a great Chablis Grand Cru on steroids.

### Producers

- **Coche-Dury** — THE Corton-Charlemagne. 99-100 JD. ~€2,000-5,000. If Meursault is Coche's daily driver, this is the rocket ship.
- **Bonneau du Martray** — Monopole of Corton-Charlemagne's largest single holding. Now owned by Stan Kroenke (Screaming Eagle). 95-98. ~€150-300.
- **Louis Latour** — Large producer but their Corton-Charlemagne is a benchmark. ~€80-150. 93-96.
- **Bouchard Père** — Excellent, large holdings. 93-96. ~€80-150.
- **Domaine Rapet** — Value pick. 92-95. ~€60-100.
- **Joseph Drouhin** — Reliable. 93-95. ~€80-120.
- **Faiveley** — Improving. 92-95. ~€80-120.

## MÂCONNAIS (VALUE WHITE BURGUNDY)

### Why It Matters

South of the Côte de Beaune. Same grape (Chardonnay), warmer climate, less prestige — but the quality revolution is real. Best producers rival village Meursault at 1/3 the price.

### Appellations

- **Pouilly-Fuissé** — Now has Premier Cru status (since 2020!). Rich, stone fruit, mineral. Best: Château de Fuissé, Domaine Ferret, Daniel & Julien Barraud, Domaine Robert-Denogent. 1er Cru ~€25-45. Village ~€15-25. 90-94.
- **Saint-Véran** — Adjacent to Pouilly-Fuissé. Similar but slightly lighter. €12-20. 89-92. Good everyday.
- **Viré-Clessé** — Rich, ripe, sometimes a touch of residual. Domaine de la Bongran (legendary). €15-25.
- **Mâcon-Villages** — Entry. Fresh, citrus. €8-15. Everyday white.

### The Mâcon Secret (per Hugues Forget / TGL)

"In Burgundy where everything is expensive, Mâconnais remains. Plus, Hautes-Côtes-de-Beaune and Hautes-Côtes-de-Nuits — top vignerons are planting there now, climate change helps." Budget Burgundy that doesn't taste like budget.

## KELLY'S WHITE BURGUNDY PLAYBOOK

### By Budget

- **Under €20:** Mâcon-Villages, Saint-Véran, good Bourgogne Blanc (Roulot, Leflaive)
- **€20-50:** Pouilly-Fuissé 1er Cru, Chassagne village, Meursault village (Javillier, Rémi Jobard)
- **€50-100:** Meursault 1er Cru (Charmes, Genevrières), Puligny village (Sauzet, Pernot), Chassagne 1er Cru
- **€100-300:** Meursault Perrières (Lafon, Roulot, Jobard), Puligny Pucelles (Leflaive), Corton-Charlemagne (Bonneau du Martray)
- **€300+:** Montrachet, Chevalier-Montrachet, Coche-Dury anything

### By Style

- **Mineral, taut, intellectual:** Puligny (Leflaive Pucelles), Corton-Charlemagne (Bonneau du Martray), Chablis Grand Cru
- **Rich, generous, nutty:** Meursault Charmes (Lafon), Chassagne Morgeot (Ramonet)
- **The "honorary Grand Cru":** Meursault Perrières — everyone agrees it should be Grand Cru. It isn't. Buy it.
- **The insider flex:** Bourgogne Blanc from a top domaine. Roulot Bourgogne Blanc (~€25-35) is better than most village wines from lesser producers. Same with Leflaive Bourgogne Blanc (~€30-40).

### The One Rule

**Buy the producer, not the appellation.** A Roulot village Meursault destroys a mediocre Puligny Premier Cru. The domaine name on the label matters more than the vineyard name.
