# Wine Regions: When to Recommend What (France-focused)

Quick reference for matching occasion, mood, and food to the right French region. Use this to steer recommendations and then pull detail from the specific wine-tasting notes.

## By Occasion

- **Celebration / apéritif / toasting:** Champagne. Crémant de Loire for value bubbles.
- **Romantic or refined dinner (red):** Bordeaux (Margaux, Saint-Émilion, Pomerol) or Burgundy (Côte de Nuits, Côte de Beaune). Northern Rhône (Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie) for power.
- **Romantic or refined dinner (white):** Pessac-Léognan / Graves white (Château Olivier, Domaine de Chevalier), White Burgundy (Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet, Chablis).
- **Weekend lunch, seafood, outdoor:** White Bordeaux (Pessac-Léognan), Chablis, Sancerre, Muscadet, Vouvray sec.
- **Steak, lamb, game, hearty food:** Red Bordeaux (Margaux, Médoc), Northern Rhône (Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie, Crozes-Hermitage), Southern Rhône (Châteauneuf-du-Pape).
- **Light red, chillable:** Beaujolais cru (Fleurie, Morgon), Chinon, Bourgueil.
- **Dessert, foie gras, blue cheese:** Sauternes, Barsac.
- **Asian or spicy food:** Alsace (Riesling, Gewurztraminer).
- **Something different from Bordeaux:** Burgundy (red or white), Northern or Southern Rhône, Loire, Beaujolais.

## By Style

- **Classic, age-worthy red:** Bordeaux (Margaux, Pauillac, Saint-Émilion), Northern Rhône (Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie), Burgundy (Côte de Nuits).
- **Elegant, aromatic red:** Margaux, Burgundy Pinot Noir, Saint-Émilion.
- **Structured, spicy red:** Northern Rhône Syrah (Hermitage, Crozes-Hermitage), Southern Rhône (Châteauneuf-du-Pape).
- **Dry, mineral white:** Graves / Pessac-Léognan white, Chablis, Sancerre, Muscadet.
- **Richer white, still dry:** White Burgundy (Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet), fuller Pessac-Léognan, Condrieu.
- **Aromatic white:** Alsace (Riesling, Gewurztraminer, Pinot Gris).
- **Sweet white:** Sauternes, Barsac.
- **Bubbles:** Champagne; Crémant de Loire for value.
- **Light, fruity red:** Beaujolais cru, Loire Cabernet Franc (Chinon, Bourgueil).

## By guest preference

Use the **sommelier-playbook** (tasting language, pairing logic, service) for full guest-led logic. Quick routing:

- **Don't like oaky:** Chablis, Sancerre, Muscadet, unoaked or light-oak whites (Vouvray sec, village Chablis).
- **Want value:** Mâcon, Saint-Véran, Crozes-Hermitage, Beaujolais cru, Gigondas, Vacqueyras, Muscadet, Crémant de Loire.
- **Want to impress:** Grand cru or premier cru Burgundy; first growth Bordeaux; prestige Champagne; Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie; Yquem or top Sauternes.
- **Something mineral:** Chablis, Sancerre, Pouilly-Fumé, Pessac-Léognan white, Muscadet.
- **Aromatic / not boring:** Alsace (Riesling, Gewurztraminer, Pinot Gris), Condrieu.
- **Having duck:** Red Burgundy (Volnay, Gevrey, Vosne) or Saint-Émilion.
- **Having lamb / steak:** Bordeaux (Margaux, Pauillac) or Northern Rhône (Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie).

## Rule of thumb

Lead with one French region and one concrete appellation or wine from the-good-life when possible. Use the **sommelier-playbook** for precise tasting language, pairing rationale, service (temperature, decanting), and guest preference mapping. If the ask is vague ("something nice for dinner"), use occasion and food to narrow. If the specific wine or domaine isn't in knowledge, use web search (WEB_SEARCH) and say you looked it up, or suggest the region and MICHELIN Guide / James Edition.
