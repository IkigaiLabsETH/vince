# Les seconds vins & assimilés — Bordeaux Second Wines

Curated list of **second wines** (and similar cuvées) from Bordeaux châteaux for Kelly. Second wines are made from younger vines or declassified lots from the grand vin; they offer the house style at a lower price and often drink earlier. Use when the user asks for "second wine," "baby Margaux," "Pavillon Rouge," "Petit Mouton," "Pagodes de Cos," or a bottle from this list. See also **bordeaux-overview.md**, **margaux.md**, **pessac-leognan.md**.

---

## Haut-Médoc

- **Goulée by Cos d'Estournel 2007** — **Second label** of Cos d'Estournel (Saint-Estèphe); Goulée is from the Médoc property. Cabernet-led, earlier-drinking than the grand vin. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C; lamb, steak.

---

## Pessac-Léognan

- **Clémentin de Pape-Clément 2009** — **Second wine** of Château Pape-Clément. 2009 in a good window; structured, polished. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Clémentin de Pape-Clément 2007** — Same; 2007 drinking now.
- **La Réserve de Malartic 2007** — **Second wine** of Château Malartic-Lagravière. 2007 drinking now; decant 30 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Bahans de Haut-Brion — Magnum 2000** — **Second wine** of Château Haut-Brion (renamed **Clarence de Haut-Brion** from 2007). 2000 magnum ages slowly; drinking well. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C; landmark second wine. *Note: 2000 vintage = still "Bahans" on label.*

---

## Saint-Émilion Grand Cru

- **N°3 d'Angelus 2011** — **Second wine** of Château Angélus (premier grand cru classé A). 2011 drinking now or short hold; Merlot-led, silky. Decant 30 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Le Carillon d'Angélus 2010** — Another **second-tier** cuvée from Angélus. 2010 in a good window; 16–18 °C.
- **N°3 d'Angelus — Double Magnum 2008** — Same second wine; **double magnum** (3 L) format; 2008 drinking now. Decant 45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Virginie de Valandraud 2005** — **Second wine** of Château Valandraud (garagiste icon). 2005 structured; decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Arômes de Pavie 2005** — **Second wine** of Château Pavie (premier grand cru classé A). 2005 powerful; decant 45 min; 16–18 °C; lamb, game.

---

## Saint-Estèphe

- **Pagodes de Cos 2004** — **Second wine** of Cos d'Estournel. 2004 in a good window; Cabernet-led, firm. Decant 45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Pagodes de Cos 2003** — Same; 2003 hot vintage; drinking now. 16–18 °C.

---

## Pauillac

- **La Réserve de la Comtesse 2009** — **Second wine** of Pichon Longueville Comtesse de Lalande (2nd growth). 2009 elegant, approachable; decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Le Petit Mouton 2008** — **Second wine** of Château Mouton Rothschild (1st growth). 2008 in a good window; rich, Pauillac power. Decant 45 min; 16–18 °C; landmark second wine.
- **La Réserve de la Comtesse 2007** — Same as above; 2007 drinking now. 16–18 °C.
- **Les Hauts de Pontet-Canet 2005** — **Second wine** of Château Pontet-Canet (5th growth, biodynamic). 2005 structured; decant 45 min; 16–18 °C.

---

## Margaux

- **Le Chevalier de Lascombes 2009** — **Second wine** of Château Lascombes (Margaux). 2009 drinking now; decant 30 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Le Pavillon Rouge du Château Margaux 2006** — **Second wine** of Château Margaux (1st growth). One of the most sought-after second wines in Bordeaux. 2006 in a good window; elegant, perfumed. Decant 45–60 min; 16–18 °C; landmark second wine.

## When to use this list

- **"Second wine" / "baby [château]" / value from a great house:** Lead with Pavillon Rouge (Margaux), Petit Mouton (Mouton), Bahans/Clarence (Haut-Brion), Pagodes de Cos, Réserve de la Comtesse, Clémentin de Pape-Clément, or N°3 d'Angelus.
- **Specific bottle from this list:** Use the entry above; for exact drinking window, use WEB_SEARCH if needed.

## Service (summary)

- **Reds:** 16–18 °C. Decant young, tannic second wines (Pauillac, Saint-Estèphe) 30–45 min; Right Bank (Saint-Émilion) 30 min. Older bottles (2000, 2003, 2004, 2005) decant briefly or 30–45 min to open.
