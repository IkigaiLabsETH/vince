# Condrieu, Northern Rhône, France — Beyond Expert

## Overview

**Condrieu** is a small AOC in the Northern Rhône; **Viognier only**. White wine: floral (violet, acacia), stone fruit (apricot, peach), often a touch of exotic (mango, ginger); rich texture but **dry**. Very limited acreage; production is small and **premium**. One of the world's reference regions for Viognier. No red wine in the AOC (reds are Côte-Rôtie next door).

## Terroir and geography

- **Location:** Steep slopes along the right bank of the Rhône, south of Vienne; communes include Condrieu, Chavanay, Saint-Michel-sur-Rhône, Vérin, and others. Granitic and gneiss soils; steep, south-facing; low yields.
- **Climate:** Continental with Mediterranean influence; Viognier needs warmth and sun to ripen; harvest is late. Frost and hail are risks.
- **Why it's special:** Viognier is fragile and low-yielding; Condrieu's slopes and soils give concentration and aromatic intensity without losing freshness when picked at the right time. Overripe = flabby; underripe = green. Top producers pick at optimal maturity and often use large oak or concrete for texture without heavy toast.

## Style and profile

- **Aromatics:** Violet, acacia, white flowers; apricot, peach, nectarine; sometimes ginger, white pepper, almond. **No oak dominance** in the best examples; oak is for texture and roundness.
- **Palate:** Medium to full body; rich, oily texture; dry (no residual sugar in classic Condrieu); acidity can be moderate — the best have enough tension to stay fresh. **Drink relatively young** (2–8 years) for primary fruit; some age 10+ years and develop honey and dried fruit.
- **Common mistakes:** Over-oaked (masks aromatics); over-cropped (thin); late-harvest sweet versions (Condrieu can be vinified in different styles; dry is the classic).

## Producers to know (beyond expert)

- **Yves Cuilleron:** Reference producer; multiple lieu-dits (Les Chaillets, Vernon, etc.); rich but precise; often 90+ scores. Top tier.
- **André Perret:** Condrieu and Saint-Joseph; Condrieu Clos Chanson, Coteau de Chéry; floral, stone fruit, fine. Consistently high regard.
- **François Villard:** Condrieu Les Terrasses du Palat, Deponcins; aromatic, textured. Often 90+.
- **Pierre Gaillard:** Condrieu; floral, peach; reliable.
- **Guigal:** La Doriane (single-vineyard Condrieu); rich, age-worthy; prestige cuvée. Condrieu standard bottling also very good.
- **Domaine Georges Vernay:** Historic estate; Coteau de Vernon; classic, fine. One of the fathers of modern Condrieu.
- **Jean-Michel Gerin:** Condrieu; ripe, floral.
- **Christine Vernay (Georges Vernay):** Continues the estate; Vernon, Terrasses de l'Empire.
- **Domaine du Monteillet (Stéphane Montez):** Condrieu; small production; highly regarded.
- **Nicolas Fafournoux:** Condrieu; precise, aromatic.

## Vineyards and lieu-dits

- **Coteau de Vernon:** Steep, granite; one of the most famous sites; Vernay, Cuilleron, Villard.
- **Chaillets, Vernon, Coteau de Chéry:** Names to look for on labels; indicate specific slopes and soils.
- Many producers blend several parcels; single-vineyard bottlings command a premium and show site character.

## Vintage (short)

Condrieu is drink-young for fruit; 2–6 years typical. Strong vintages (2015, 2016, 2019, 2020) can age 8–12 years for top cuvées. Avoid very hot years if you want tension; 2017, 2019, 2020 balanced. For "drink now" suggest 2020 or 2021.

## Service

- **Temperature:** 10–12 °C. Too cold mutes flowers and stone fruit; too warm feels heavy.
- **Glass:** Large white or Viognier glass to capture aroma.
- **Decanting:** Optional 15–20 min for richer cuvées.

## Food pairings

- **Classic:** Lobster, scallops, crab; rich fish (monkfish, turbot) with butter or cream; poultry (roast chicken, guinea fowl); foie gras (dry Condrieu works: aromatic and rich). Asian-inspired (ginger, coconut, mild spice) — Viognier's aromatics and texture pair well.
- **Avoid:** Very light dishes (wine overwhelms); very spicy heat (alcohol can amplify). Match weight: rich food, rich wine.

## When to recommend (guest-led)

- **"Aromatic white, not Chardonnay"** → Condrieu; "Viognier, floral and stone fruit, Northern Rhône; Cuilleron, Perret, Villard."
- **"Something special, white"** → Condrieu single-vineyard (Cuilleron Vernon, Vernay Coteau de Vernon, Guigal La Doriane).
- **"White for lobster or scallops"** → Condrieu; "rich texture and aromatics match the shellfish."
- **"Best Viognier"** → Condrieu; name Cuilleron, Vernay, Perret, Villard; "the world reference for Viognier."
