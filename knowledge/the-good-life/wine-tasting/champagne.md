# Champagne

## When to Recommend

- **Celebrations:** Anniversaries, launches, toasts, “something special.”
- **Apéritif:** Before dinner; works with canapés, oysters, light bites.
- **Brunch or lunch:** Lighter occasions; pairs with oysters, seafood, fried or salty snacks.
- **Gift or gesture:** Bottle as a thank-you or host gift.

## Style and Terms

- **Blend:** Usually Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier. Blanc de blancs = Chardonnay only (lighter, citrus, fine bubbles). Blanc de noirs = Pinot (more body, structure).
- **Profile:** Dry (brut), crisp, citrus and brioche, fine bubbles. Vintage = single year (more character, age-worthy); non-vintage (NV) = multi-year blend (consistent, often best value).
- **Prestige cuvées:** Top bottlings from grandes marques (e.g. Dom Pérignon, Krug, Cristal, etc.) — for splashy occasions; check current availability and price.

## Food Pairings

Oysters, shellfish, smoked fish, gougères, fried or salty snacks, soft cheese, light canapés. Avoid heavy or sweet dishes so the wine stays fresh.

## Producers to know

- **Grandes marques (houses):** Moët & Chandon, Veuve Clicquot, Ruinart, Taittinger, Louis Roederer (Cristal), Dom Pérignon (Moët), Krug, Bollinger, Pol Roger, Perrier-Jouët. Prestige: Dom Pérignon, Krug Grande Cuvée / Krug vintage, Cristal, Comtes de Champagne (Taittinger), Sir Winston Churchill (Pol Roger).
- **Grower Champagne (récoltant-manipulant):** Egly-Ouriet, Pierre Péters, Jacques Selosse, Larmandier-Bernier, Agrapart, Vouette & Sorbée. Often more terroir-driven; good for something different.

## Vintage (short)

Vintage Champagne: 2008, 2012, 2013 are excellent; 2014, 2015 good. Drink NV within a few years of release for freshness; vintage and prestige 10–20+ years. For drink now lead with NV brut.

## Service

- **Temperature:** 6–8 °C. Do not overchill (mutes flavor). Ice bucket 15–20 min is enough.
- **Glass:** Flute or tulip; tulip preferred by many sommeliers for aroma. Open without pop; twist base, hold cork.

## Guest-led

- If they want **value** → NV brut from a good house (Taittinger, Pol Roger) or grower (Péters, Larmandier-Bernier).
- If they want **to impress** → Prestige cuvée (Dom Pérignon, Krug, Cristal) or top grower (Selosse, Egly-Ouriet).
- If they want **mineral, refined** → Blanc de blancs (Chardonnay): Ruinart, Taittinger Comtes, Péters, Agrapart.
- If they're having **oysters** → Brut NV or blanc de blancs; acidity and salt pair perfectly.
