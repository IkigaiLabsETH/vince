# Les vins rouges de Provence — Bandol & Curated List

Curated list of **Provence** red wines for Kelly: **Bandol** (Mourvèdre-dominant). Use when the user asks for "Bandol," "Provence red," "Tempier," "Pibarnon," "La Bastide Blanche," or a bottle from this list. See also **regions.md**. Bandol is one of the finest red appellations of Provence; wines are structured, age-worthy, and pair well with lamb, daube, and Mediterranean food.

---

## Bandol (Mourvèdre-led)

Bandol AOC (Var, Provence) requires a minimum of 50% Mourvèdre for reds; top estates use 75–95%. Reds are tannic when young, age 10–20+ years; flavours of dark fruit, garrigue, spice.

- **Domaine Tempier 2011** — **Bandol**; Tempier is the reference estate, iconic. 2011 drinking now or short hold; Mourvèdre-led, structured, garrigue. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C; lamb, daube, grilled meat, Mediterranean.
- **Château de Pibarnon 2010** — **Bandol**; Pibarnon is a top estate (high altitude, limestone). 2010 in a good window; elegant, mineral. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C; lamb, herbs, ratatouille.
- **La Bastide Blanche 2008** — **Bandol**; La Bastide Blanche is a leading estate. 2008 drinking now; structured, red fruit, garrigue. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **La Bastide Blanche 2006** — Same estate; 2006 mature; 16–18 °C; decant 30 min.
- **La Bastide Blanche 2004** — Same estate; 2004 in a good window; 16–18 °C; decant 30–45 min.
