# Les vins blancs secs de Bordeaux — Dry White Wines of Bordeaux

Curated list of Bordeaux wines for Kelly: dry whites by sub-region, plus a few reds from the same list where noted. Use this when the user asks for "Bordeaux white," "dry white Bordeaux," "Graves white," "Pessac-Léognan white," or for a specific château from this list.

## General Bordeaux (generic Bordeaux AOC or multi-appellation)

- **Château des Milles Anges 2012** — Dry white; drink now or short hold.
- **Château Guiraud 2013** — Guiraud is best known for Sauternes; they also produce **G de Guiraud** (dry white). If the bottle is G de Guiraud 2013: fresh, dry, citrus; good with shellfish. Serve 10–12 °C.
- **Domaine de L'Alliance 2013** — Dry white Bordeaux; classic Sauvignon/Sémillon profile.
- **Château Monbousquet 2010** — *Red* (Saint-Émilion). Merlot-led; rich, plush. Decant 30–45 min; 16–18 °C.
- **Cos d'Estournel 2009** — *Red* (Saint-Estèphe, 2nd growth). Cabernet-dominant; powerful, structured, long-lived. Decant 1–2 hours; 16–18 °C.
- **Pavillon blanc de Château Margaux 2003** — **Dry white** from Château Margaux (Sauvignon Blanc). Rare, age-worthy, mineral and citrus; one of the most sought-after dry whites of Bordeaux. Drink now or hold; 10–12 °C. Pair with fine fish, shellfish, or goat cheese.

## Entre-Deux-Mers

Dry whites from the large Entre-Deux-Mers AOC (between the Dordogne and Garonne). Often Sauvignon-dominant; fresh, good value, everyday drinking. Serve 8–10 °C.

- **Château Turcaud 2013** — Entre-Deux-Mers dry white.
- **Château Clos Chaumont 2012** — Entre-Deux-Mers dry white.
- **Château Tour Mirambeau 2014** — Entre-Deux-Mers dry white.

## Graves

**Graves** (outside Pessac-Léognan) produces both red and white; whites are dry, often good value.

- **Château Chantegrive — Cuvée Caroline 2013** — **Dry white** Graves; named cuvée, step up in quality. Citrus, white flowers, mineral; good with seafood or aperitif. 10–12 °C.

## Pessac-Léognan

**Pessac-Léognan** is the prestige AOC for Graves; many estates produce both red and **dry white**. Whites: Sémillon + Sauvignon; mineral, citrus, waxy; age-worthy. See also **pessac-leognan.md** and **chateau-olivier.md**.

- **Château Pape Clément — Grand Cru Classé de Graves 2011** — Red and white produced; white is serious, mineral. Red: structured, classed growth; decant 45–90 min.
- **Domaine de Chevalier — Grand Cru Classé de Graves 2010** — **Red and white.** White: among the best dry whites of Bordeaux; refined, mineral, long-lived. Red: elegant, age-worthy. 90–96+ in top vintages. See pessac-leognan.md.
- **Château Malartic-Lagravière — Grand Cru Classé de Graves 2010** — Red and white; reliable, mineral. White: citrus, wet stone; good with fish and shellfish.
- **Château de Fieuzal 2008** — Red and white; red is classed-growth level. White: dry, precise, mineral.
- **Château Latour-Martillac — Grand Cru Classé de Graves 2010** — *Do not confuse with Château Latour (Pauillac).* Latour-Martillac is Pessac-Léognan; red and white; classic Graves profile.
- **Château Carbonnieux — Grand Cru Classé de Graves 2010** — Large estate; red and **white**; value and quality, widely available. White: fresh, mineral, citrus; 88–92+.
- **Château Haut-Brion 1995** — **First growth (1855) and classed Graves.** Red and white; both among the world's greatest. **1995 red:** drinking window now; refined, complex, long-lived. **Haut-Brion blanc:** dense, mineral, age-worthy; 95–100 in top vintages. Reference for Pessac-Léognan. Decant red 45–60 min; white 10–12 °C.

## When to use this list

- **"Bordeaux dry white" / "Graves white" / "Pessac-Léognan white":** Lead with Domaine de Chevalier blanc, Château Olivier blanc, Haut-Brion blanc, or Chantegrive Cuvée Caroline; then Carbonnieux, Malartic-Lagravière, Latour-Martillac, Fieuzal, Pape Clément blanc. For **value:** Entre-Deux-Mers (Turcaud, Clos Chaumont, Tour Mirambeau).
- **"Something special" / landmark white:** Pavillon blanc de Château Margaux, Haut-Brion blanc, Domaine de Chevalier blanc.
- **Specific bottle from this list:** Use the entry above; for vintage or score, add WEB_SEARCH if needed.

## Service (dry whites)

- **Temperature:** 10–12 °C for Pessac-Léognan / Graves; 8–10 °C for Entre-Deux-Mers (lighter, more quaffable).
- **Pairing:** Shellfish, fish, goat cheese, aperitif, refined lunch. Do not overchill; mineral and citrus show better when not ice-cold.
