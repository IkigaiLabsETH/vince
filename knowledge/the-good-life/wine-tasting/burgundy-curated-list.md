## Les vins blancs de Bourgogne (White Wines of Burgundy)

### Bourgogne (generic white)

- **Domaine Vincent Girardin — Cuvée Saint-Vincent 2012** — Bourgogne blanc; Chardonnay, fresh, citrus; good value. Drink now; 10–12 °C. Pair with light fish, aperitif.

### Chablis

- **Joseph Drouhin — Grand Cru « Les Clos » 2012** — Chablis grand cru; Les Clos is the most revered climat. Steely, mineral, long; benchmark. 10–12 °C; oysters, shellfish, goat cheese.
- **Domaine Dauvissat — Premier Cru « La Forest » 2011** — Dauvissat is a reference producer; La Forest premier cru. Mineral, precise, age-worthy. 9–11 °C.
- **Domaine Raveneau — Premier Cru « Forêt » 2004** — Raveneau is among the greatest Chablis producers; Forêt (or Forêts) premier cru. Rare, mineral, long-lived. 10–12 °C; drink now or short hold.

### Beaune (white)

- **Joseph Drouhin — Premier Cru « Clos des Mouches » 2011** — Clos des Mouches is a famous Drouhin monopole; **red and white** produced. White: refined, citrus, stone fruit; Côte de Beaune style. 10–12 °C; poultry, lobster, cream sauces.

### Meursault

- **Domaine Vincent Girardin — Premier Cru « Les Perrières » 2011** — Perrières is one of Meursault’s top premiers crus; mineral, tension, stone fruit. 10–12 °C.
- **Domaine des Comtes Lafon — Premier Cru « Genevrières » 2003** — Lafon is a reference for Meursault; Genevrières is powerful, nutty, age-worthy. 2003 was hot; drink now. 10–12 °C; poultry, lobster, truffle.

### Puligny-Montrachet

- **Domaine Leflaive — Premier Cru « Les Pucelles » 2010** — Leflaive is a benchmark for Puligny; Les Pucelles is floral, fine, mineral. 90–95+ in top vintages. 10–12 °C; shellfish, roast chicken.
- **Louis Jadot — Folatières — Premier Cru 2009** — Puligny-Montrachet premier cru Folatières; Jadot négociant. Classic Puligny: tension, citrus, white flowers. 10–12 °C.

### Bâtard-Montrachet (grand cru)

- **Domaine Louis Jadot — Grand Cru 2004** — Bâtard-Montrachet grand cru; rich, powerful, long-lived. One of the great white grands crus. 10–12 °C; lobster, truffle, fine dining.

### Montrachet (grand cru)

- **Domaine Leflaive — Montrachet 2007** — Montrachet grand cru; the pinnacle of white Burgundy. Leflaive’s parcel is tiny; rare, profound. 10–12 °C; landmark wine.
- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — Montrachet 2004** — DRC’s Montrachet; among the most sought-after white wines in the world. 95–100 tier; serve 10–12 °C for a landmark occasion.

### Chassagne-Montrachet

- **Domaine Joseph Drouhin — Marquis de Laguiche 2012** — Drouhin’s Marquis de Laguiche is a historic holding in Chassagne (including Montrachet parcels); this cuvée is Chassagne-level. Refined, mineral. 10–12 °C.
- **Domaine Joseph Drouhin — Marquis de Laguiche 2011** — Same estate; 2011 vintage; drink now.
- **Domaine Louis Jadot — Premier Cru « Grandes Ruchottes » 2004** — Chassagne-Montrachet premier cru; Grandes Ruchottes is a strong climat. Rich, mineral; 10–12 °C.

## Les vins rouges de Bourgogne (Red Wines of Burgundy)

### Bourgogne (generic red)

- **Domaine Vincent Girardin — Cuvée Saint-Vincent 2011** — Bourgogne rouge; Pinot Noir, fruity, approachable. Good value; 14–16 °C; drink now.

### Morey-Saint-Denis

- **Domaine du Clos des Lambrays — Grand Cru — Magnum 1999** — Clos des Lambrays grand cru (now often written Clos des Lambrays); magnum ages more slowly. 1999 is in a good drinking window. Silky, red fruit, spice. Decant 30 min; 15–16 °C.

### Chambolle-Musigny

- **Domaine Joseph Drouhin — Premier Cru 2011** — Chambolle-Musigny premier cru; Drouhin’s Chambolle is often from Beaux Bruns or other 1er crus. Fragrant, delicate, red fruit. 14–15 °C; duck, poultry.

### Gevrey-Chambertin

- **Domaine Louis Jadot — Premier Cru « Clos Saint-Jacques » 2004** — Clos Saint-Jacques is one of Gevrey’s finest premiers crus (many consider it grand cru level). Structured, dark fruit, spice. Decant 30–45 min; 15–16 °C.
- **Domaine Armand Rousseau — Premier Cru « Les Cazetiers » 2007** — Rousseau is a reference for Gevrey; Les Cazetiers premier cru. Elegant, precise, long-lived. 15–16 °C.
- **Domaine Armand Rousseau 2011** — Gevrey-Chambertin village (or blend); Rousseau’s village is serious. Drink now or short hold; 15–16 °C.

### Bonnes-Mares (grand cru)

- **Domaine Georges Roumier — Grand Cru 2004** — Bonnes-Mares grand cru (Chambolle-Musigny side); Roumier is legendary. Dense, mineral, age-worthy. Decant 45 min; 15–16 °C.

### Echezeaux (grand cru)

- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — Echezeaux Grand Cru 2007** — DRC Echezeaux; silky, floral, profound. 15–16 °C; decant 30–45 min.
- **Domaine Louis Jadot — Echezeaux Grand Cru 2006** — Jadot’s Echezeaux; classic Vosne/Nuits grand cru style. 15–16 °C.
- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — Echezeaux Grand Cru 2004** — DRC Echezeaux 2004; drink now or hold.

### Grands Echezeaux (grand cru)

- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — Grands Echezeaux Grand Cru 2003** — DRC Grands Echezeaux; step up from Echezeaux in concentration. 2003 was hot; likely mature. 15–16 °C.

### Romanée-Saint-Vivant (grand cru)

- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — Romanée-Saint-Vivant Grand Cru 1991** — DRC RSV; one of the great grands crus of Vosne. 1991 is mature; silk, sous-bois, red fruit. Decant briefly; 15–16 °C.

### Richebourg (grand cru)

- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — Richebourg Grand Cru 1996** — DRC Richebourg; powerful, profound. 1996 is structured, long-lived. Decant 45 min; 15–16 °C.

### La Tâche (grand cru)

- **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — La Tâche Grand Cru 2002** — DRC La Tâche (monopole); among the greatest red wines of Burgundy. 2002 is excellent; drinking window now. Decant 45–60 min; 15–16 °C.

### Clos de Vougeot (grand cru)

- **Domaine Louis Jadot — Clos de Vougeot Grand Cru 2007** — Clos de Vougeot grand cru; large climat, variable; Jadot’s is reliable. Structured, dark fruit. 15–16 °C; decant 30–45 min.

### Beaune (red)

- **Domaine Joseph Drouhin — Premier Cru « Clos des Mouches » 1996** — Clos des Mouches red; Drouhin monopole. 1996 is mature; red fruit, silk. Decant briefly; 14–15 °C.

### Pommard

- **Domaine de Montille — Premier Cru « Les Rugiens » 1998** — Rugiens is Pommard’s top premier cru; Montille is a reference. Firm, structured, age-worthy. 1998 drinking now; 15–16 °C.
- **Maison Leroy 1964** — Leroy Pommard (village or premier cru); 1964 is very old. Decant briefly only; serve 14–15 °C; handle with care.

### Volnay

- **Domaine Vincent Girardin — Premier Cru « Les Champans » 2011** — Volnay premier cru Champans; delicate, red fruit, floral. 14–15 °C; duck, poultry.
- **Domaine de Montille — Premier Cru « Les Taillepieds » 1998** — Volnay Taillepieds premier cru; Montille. Elegant, fine; 1998 drinking now. 14–15 °C.
- **Domaine de Montille — Premier Cru « Les Mitans » 1997** — Volnay Les Mitans premier cru; 1997 vintage drinking now. 14–15 °C.

## When to use this list

- **White Burgundy / Chablis / Meursault / Puligny / Montrachet:** Use the "Vins blancs" section; lead with village or cru (e.g. Leflaive Pucelles, DRC Montrachet, Raveneau Forêt, Drouhin Les Clos).
- **Red Burgundy / Gevrey / Vosne / DRC / Volnay / Pommard:** Use the "Vins rouges" section; lead with cru or domaine (e.g. DRC La Tâche, Rousseau, Roumier Bonnes-Mares, Montille Volnay).
- **Specific bottle from this list:** Use the entry above; for exact drinking window or score, use WEB_SEARCH if needed.

## Service (summary)

- **Whites:** 10–12 °C (Chablis 8–10 °C for village). Do not overchill.
- **Reds:** 14–16 °C (Côte de Nuits 15–16 °C). Decant young, structured reds 30–45 min; old or delicate reds briefly or not at all.
