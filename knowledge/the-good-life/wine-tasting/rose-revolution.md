# Rosé Revolution — Beyond Summer Sipping

Rosé is no longer a seasonal throwaway. Top Provence domaines now produce age-worthy rosés (rosés de garde) that rival serious reds and whites. Kelly should know this shift.

## The Shift: Rosé de Garde

- **What changed:** Top producers now hold back vintages 2-5 years before release. Bandol leads — Domaine de Terrebrune offers 2019, 2017, even 2015 rosés. 20-year verticals are a thing.
- **Why it matters:** Rosé de garde develops complexity — dried fruit, caramel, depth — like aged white Burgundy. Not just "cold pink stuff."
- **Who's driving it:** Association Internationale des Rosés de Terroir (founded 2021, Tavel). Led by Philippe Guigal (Château d'Aqueria, Tavel). Members include Bandol (Terrebrune, Pibarnon), Languedoc, Bordeaux, Sancerre, Champagne (Les Riceys), plus Italy and Lebanon.

## Provence — The Domaines to Know

### Bandol (Age-Worthy Rosé Capital)

- **Domaine de Terrebrune** — The reference for rosé de garde. Copropriétaire Jean d'Arthuys (ex-M6 group CEO). Releases old vintages to restaurants. Slightly amber color, vivacity, typicity. Smallest of Var's 4 appellations.
- **Château de Pibarnon** — Grand Bandol estate. Rosé and red both age-worthy.
- **Domaine Tempier** — Mourvèdre-led, icon estate. Already in Kelly's provence-curated-list.md.
- **La Bégude** — Rising star in Bandol.

### Côtes de Provence / Var

- **Château La Coste** — Puy-Sainte-Réparade, near Aix. Irish owner Patrick McKillen. 120ha, bio + biodynamie. Grand Rosé: cab sauv/grenache/syrah from oldest parcels up to 400m altitude. Also an art center (Tadao Ando, Jean Nouvel), 6 restaurants, 2 hotels. Coffret L'Intégrale Rosé includes Nooh (sans alcool).
- **Minuty** — Presqu'île de Saint-Tropez, cru classé. Now LVMH-owned (since ~2024). Cuvée Or = signature (grenache base, aromatic, elegant). Huge international distribution.
- **Ultimate Provence** — La Garde-Freinet. British owner Mark Dixon (Regus). Design hotel + restaurant in garrigue. Single cuvée, contemporary bottle (no label, strie design). Partnered with Q de Bouteilles for recycled glassware.
- **Sainte-Roseline** — Les Arcs-sur-Argens. Cru classé since 1955. Owned by Teillaud family since 1994 (pioneer). Summer art expo, Les Musicales festival, DJ Thursday nights. New cuvée Collection. 25% sold direct at domaine.
- **Galoupet** — Moët Hennessy property (since 2019). Cru classé. Bio certified 2023. Lightest bottle ever: 300g, 85% recycled glass, no aluminum capsule. Cuvée G Maison Galoupet. Faces Porquerolles and Île du Levant. Biodiversity lab: 200 beehives, queen-bee fecundation station.
- **La Commanderie de Peyrassol** — Owner Philippe Austruy (since 2001). 850ha total (including woods/garrigue). Cuvée 1204 (€77) — named for 1204 Templar mention and limited bottle count. Open-air art collection: Niki de Saint Phalle, Daniel Buren, Bernar Venet, Anish Kapoor.
- **Château La Gordonne** — Massif des Maures. 310ha d'un seul tenant (single holding). Maître de chai Julien Fort works microterroirs for special cuvées.
- **Château des Sarrins** — Haut-Var. Owner Bruno Paillard (Champagne Bruno Paillard). 27ha bio, vineyards = clearings in woods. Cuvée Secret: barrel-aged rosé, sold on 2021 vintage. Garde approach.
- **Château La Mascaronne** — Owner Michel Reybier (also Cos d'Estournel, Saint-Estèphe). Exclusive estate-grown (no négoce). Detailed terroir mapping. Focus: freshness + aromatics + naturalness for garde.
- **Château d'Esclans** — Moët Hennessy. Whispering Angel = the global rosé phenomenon. Top cuvées Château d'Esclans and Les Clans sold on older vintages. Cuvée Garrus was once world's most expensive rosé (€110).

### Premium Rosé Price Benchmarks

- Muse de Miraval: €240 (magnum only)
- Étoile de Ott: €130
- Garrus (Esclans): €110
- 1204 (Peyrassol): €77
- Clos du Temple (Gérard Bertrand, Languedoc): €199 — most expensive single-bottle rosé

## Tavel — The OG Rosé Appellation

- Gard, Southern Rhône. Only-rosé appellation. Deep color, structured, has always been serious.
- **Château d'Aqueria** — Philippe Guigal (yes, that Guigal). Cuvée L'Héritage: 2 years in cave before release.

## When to Recommend Rosé

- **Summer lunch/apéro:** Any quality Côtes de Provence (Minuty Or, Ultimate Provence, Galoupet G)
- **Serious dinner pairing:** Bandol rosé (Terrebrune, Pibarnon) — treat like a light red; pairs with grilled fish, Mediterranean cuisine, bouillabaisse
- **Gift / to impress:** Peyrassol 1204, Esclans Garrus, or a garde vintage from Terrebrune/Sarrins
- **Cellar-worthy:** Bandol rosé, Tavel (Aqueria L'Héritage), Sarrins Secret — hold 3-10+ years
- **No alcohol:** Château La Coste Nooh

## Service

- Fresh rosé: 8-10°C
- Rosé de garde (aged): 10-12°C (treat more like white Burgundy)
- Glass: White wine glass, not flute
