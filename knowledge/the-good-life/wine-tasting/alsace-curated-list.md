## Riesling Münchberg (Grand Cru) — dry and Vendanges Tardives

**Münchberg** is a grand cru at Nothalten/Andlau; Riesling from here is powerful, mineral, long-lived.

- **Ostertag — « Pas en vendanges tardives » 2012** — Riesling Münchberg **dry** (not late-harvest); Ostertag is a reference in Andlau. "Pas en VT" = deliberately picked for dry style. Citrus, mineral, tension. 8–10 °C; seafood, Asian, poultry, apéritif.
- **Ostertag — « En vendanges tardives » 2011** — Riesling Münchberg **Vendanges Tardives** (VT): late-harvest, sweet to semi-sweet, honey, apricot, noble. Serve 8–10 °C; foie gras, blue cheese, fruit desserts, or as an apéritif. Age-worthy.

## Riesling (Grand Cru and flagship cuvées)

- **Domaine Trimbach — Frédéric Emile — Grand Cru 2009** — Riesling from Geisberg + Osterberg (grands crus); Trimbach’s top blended Riesling. Dry, precise, mineral, long. 90–95+ in top vintages. 8–10 °C; shellfish, Asian, roast poultry.
- **Domaine Trimbach — Clos Sainte Hune — Grand Cru 2007** — **Clos Sainte Hune** (Rosacker grand cru, Hunawihr); one of the most famous dry Rieslings in the world. 2007 in a good drinking window; mineral, citrus, immense length. 8–10 °C; landmark wine; seafood, fine dining.
- **Domaine Trimbach — Clos Sainte Hune — Grand Cru 1998** — Same cuvée; 1998 mature; honey, petrol, lime; decant briefly optional. 8–10 °C.
- **Kientzler — Geisberg — Grand Cru — Magnum 1995** — Riesling Geisberg grand cru (Ribeauvillé); Kientzler is a respected estate. **Magnum** ages more slowly; 1995 in a good window. Dry, mineral, developed. 8–10 °C; shellfish, poultry, aged cheese.

## When to use this list

- **Alsace Riesling / dry Riesling:** Lead with Trimbach Frédéric Emile or Clos Sainte Hune, or Ostertag "pas en VT"; Kientzler Geisberg for grand cru from Ribeauvillé.
- **Alsace sweet / Vendanges Tardives:** Ostertag "en vendanges tardives" — foie gras, dessert, blue cheese, or apéritif.
- **Alsace Pinot Gris / rich white:** Trimbach Réserve Personnelle.
- **Landmark / "best Alsace":** Clos Sainte Hune (any vintage); serve 8–10 °C, name the vintage from this list.
- **Specific bottle from this list:** Use the entry above; for exact drinking window or score, use WEB_SEARCH if needed.

## Service (summary)

- **Dry whites (Riesling, Pinot Gris):** 8–10 °C. Do not overchill; aromatics and mineral show better when not ice-cold.
- **Vendanges Tardives:** 8–10 °C; small pours with foie gras or dessert.
