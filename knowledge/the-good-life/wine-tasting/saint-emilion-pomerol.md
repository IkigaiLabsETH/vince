# Saint-Émilion & Pomerol, Bordeaux Right Bank — Beyond Expert

## Overview

**Saint-Émilion** and **Pomerol** are the prestige Right Bank appellations of Bordeaux: **Merlot-dominant** (with Cabernet Franc and sometimes Cabernet Sauvignon). Soils: clay, limestone, sand; cooler and heavier than the Left Bank gravel — Merlot thrives. Wines are **softer, rounder, earlier-maturing** than Left Bank but can be equally profound and long-lived. No 1855 classification here; Saint-Émilion has its own classification (revised periodically); Pomerol has no official classification but has iconic names (Pétrus, Le Pin, Lafleur).

## Saint-Émilion

### Classification (simplified)

- **Premier Grand Cru Classé A:** Cheval Blanc, Ausone (plus Figeac promoted 2022). **Top tier.**
- **Premier Grand Cru Classé B:** Angelus, Pavie, Pavie Macquin, Canon, Figeac (now A), Beauséjour Duffau, etc. (list is updated; check current classification for full list.)
- **Grand Cru Classé:** Many estates; step below B.
- **Saint-Émilion Grand Cru:** Appellation level (stricter than plain Saint-Émilion); many good value wines.
- **Saint-Émilion:** Base AOC.

### Terroir

- **Limestone plateau:** Best sites; Ausone, Canon; elegance, minerality.
- **Côtes (clay-limestone slopes):** Pavie, Angelus; power and richness.
- **Graves / sand:** Cheval Blanc (has gravel); Figeac (gravel); more Cabernet Franc; finer tannins, aromatic complexity.
- **Foot of slope, sand:** Softer, earlier-drinking; value.

### Key châteaux (beyond expert)

- **Château Cheval Blanc:** Premier Grand Cru Classé A; often high Cabernet Franc; gravel; **finesse, perfume, longevity.** 90+ in strong vintages. Reference.
- **Château Ausone:** Premier Grand Cru Classé A; limestone; **power and minerality;** tiny production; 90+.
- **Château Figeac:** Premier Grand Cru Classé A (2022); gravel; high Cabernet Franc/Cabernet Sauvignon; **fresh, structured, aromatic.** 90+.
- **Château Pavie:** Premier Grand Cru Classé B; limestone; **rich, opulent;** controversial in 2000s, reined in; 90+.
- **Château Angelus:** Premier Grand Cru Classé B; **ripe, generous;** 90+.
- **Château Canon:** Premier Grand Cru Classé B; limestone; **elegant, age-worthy.** 90+.
- **Château Pavie Macquin:** Premier Grand Cru Classé B; **structured, deep.** 90+.
- **Château Beauséjour Duffau-Lagarrosse, Troplong Mondot, Larcis Ducasse, etc.:** Grand cru classé; all capable of 90+ in top vintages.
- **Château Valandraud:** "Garagiste" star; now established; **rich, modern;** 90+.
- **Château La Gaffelière, La Dominique, Clos Fourtet:** Names to know for lists and recommendations.

### Style and vintage

- **Style:** Merlot-dominant; red and black fruit, plum, often floral (violet); tannins rounder than Left Bank; can be drunk earlier (5–15 years) but top wines age 20–40+ years.
- **Vintage:** 2015, 2016, 2018, 2019, 2020 strong. For "drink now" suggest 2015 or 2016; for cellaring 2019 or 2020.

## Pomerol

### No official classification

- Pomerol has **no 1855 or formal classification**; reputation is by estate and terroir. **Clay and gravel;** Merlot excels; some Cabernet Franc (e.g. Vieux Château Certan, Lafleur).

### Key châteaux (beyond expert)

- **Château Pétrus:** Icon; **Merlot-dominant**, clay; **opulent, profound;** among the world's most expensive wines. 95–100 in top vintages.
- **Le Pin:** Tiny production; **Merlot;** ultra-premium; 95–100.
- **Château Lafleur:** Merlot + Cabernet Franc; **mineral, precise, long-lived;** 95+.
- **Vieux Château Certan:** Significant Cabernet Franc; **elegant, aromatic;** 90+.
- **Château L'Église-Clinet, La Fleur-Pétrus, Certan de May, Clinet, Gazin, Nenin, Beauregard, etc.:** All serious; many regularly 90–95+.
- **Château L'Evangile (now under Domaines Barons de Rothschild):** **Rich, polished;** 90+.
- **Château La Conseillante:** **Silky, floral;** 90+.
- **Château Trotanoy:** **Powerful, age-worthy;** 90+.

### Style and vintage

- **Style:** Dense fruit, plum, truffle, sometimes floral; **silky tannins;** earlier approachable than many Left Bank but top wines age decades.
- **Vintage:** Same strong years as Saint-Émilion; 2015, 2016, 2018, 2019, 2020. For "drink now" 2015/2016; for cellar 2019/2020.

## Service

- **Temperature:** 16–18 °C. Decant **young** (under 10 years) 45–90 min; **older** (15+ years) 30–60 min, gentle pour to leave sediment.

## Food pairings

- Lamb, duck, beef, mushroom dishes, roast poultry. Right Bank suits **rich, not over-seared** meat; Pomerol with truffle or game.
