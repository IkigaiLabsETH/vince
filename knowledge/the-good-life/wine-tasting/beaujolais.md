# Beaujolais, France

## Overview

Region south of Burgundy; **Gamay** grape. Often light, fruity, low tannin; can be **chillable** and drunk young. **Beaujolais Nouveau** (released third Thursday of November) is the simplest; **Beaujolais Villages** is a step up; **Beaujolais crus** are the best — ten named crus with distinct character.

## The ten crus (north to south)

- **Saint-Amour:** Light, floral; romantic connotation (name).
- **Juliénas:** Structured, red fruit.
- **Chénas:** Rare; firm, floral.
- **Moulin-à-Vent:** The most structured and age-worthy; "king of Beaujolais." Can age 5–10 years. Best for "serious" Beaujolais.
- **Fleurie:** Fragrant, silky, red fruit; very popular. "Queen of Beaujolais."
- **Chiroubles:** Light, floral, delicate.
- **Morgon:** Full-bodied, cherry, can age; often compared to Burgundy. **Côte du Py** is the top sub-zone.
- **Régnié:** Fruity, approachable.
- **Brouilly:** Largest cru; soft, fruity.
- **Côte de Brouilly:** More concentration than Brouilly.

## Style and when to recommend

- **Light red, chillable:** Beaujolais Villages or Fleurie, Chiroubles. Lunch, picnic, charcuterie, poultry, casual dinner.
- **More serious but still Gamay:** Morgon, Moulin-à-Vent. Can pair with lamb, duck, mushroom dishes.
- **"Something fun," "easy red," "not heavy":** Lead with a cru (Fleurie, Morgon, Moulin-à-Vent) so it's still a clear recommendation.

## Food pairings

Charcuterie, roast chicken, turkey, cold meats, lighter meat dishes, vegetarian (mushrooms, grilled vegetables). Serve slightly chilled (14–16 °C).

## Producers to know

- **Morgon:** Jean Foillard (Côte du Py), Marcel Lapierre, Jean-Paul Thévenet, Guy Breton. "Gang of Four" — natural-leaning, serious cru Beaujolais.
- **Fleurie:** Jules Desjourneys, Chignard, Yvon Métras.
- **Moulin-à-Vent:** Château des Jacques (Louis Jadot), Domaine des Terres Dorées (Jean-Paul Brun).
- **Other crus:** Domaine Dupeuble (Brouilly), Domaine de la Voûte des Crozes (Côte de Brouilly).

## Service

- **Temperature:** 12–14 °C; slightly chilled for summer or lunch. Do not serve warm.
- **Decanting:** Optional for Morgon or Moulin-à-Vent (15–30 min opens fruit); Fleurie and lighter crus can pour straight.

## Guest-led

- If they want **value, light red, chillable** → Beaujolais cru (Fleurie, Chiroubles, Régnié); name Foillard or Lapierre for Morgon if they want "serious" Gamay.
- If they want **something fun, not heavy** → Fleurie or Morgon (silky, red fruit); "serve slightly chilled with charcuterie or roast chicken."
- If they want **Beaujolais with structure** → Morgon Côte du Py (Foillard) or Moulin-à-Vent; can age 5–10 years.
