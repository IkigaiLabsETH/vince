# High-Scoring French Wines — 90+ Points Reference

Concrete wines with verified critic scores. Scores from Robert Parker/Wine Advocate (RP), Wine Spectator (WS), James Suckling (JS), Jeb Dunnuck (JD), Vinous (V), Decanter (D). When scores vary by critic, the highest reliable published score is listed. For current vintage scores not listed here, Kelly should use WEB_SEARCH.

## Understanding Scores
- **95-100:** Classic / perfect. Rare. Investment-grade or once-in-a-lifetime bottles.
- **90-94:** Outstanding. Wines of superior character. The sweet spot for serious drinking.
- **85-89:** Very good but not in the 90+ conversation.

## KELLY'S 90+ QUICK GUIDE

### By Budget
- **Under €20:** Guigal Côtes du Rhône (90 WS), Muscadet cru (emerging scores), Crémant de Loire
- **€20-50:** Guigal Crozes-Hermitage (92 WS), Cattier/Palmer Champagne NV (90-91), Beaujolais crus
- **€50-100:** Bollinger Special Cuvée (93), Clos des Papes CdP (98 but findable ~€80), Jadot village Burgundy
- **€100-250:** Krug Grande Cuvée (96), Ducru-Beaucaillou 2016 (98), Dom Pérignon 2012 (98)
- **€250+:** First growths, DRC, Pétrus, Le Pin, Salon, Krug Clos du Mesnil

### By Occasion
- **Steak dinner:** Pauillac 2016 (Lynch-Bages 97, Grand-Puy-Lacoste 94) or Hermitage
- **Seafood:** Chablis Grand Cru (Raveneau 95), Trimbach Clos Sainte Hune (97)
- **Celebration:** Dom Pérignon 2012 (98), Cristal 2014 (98), Salon 2012 (99)
- **To impress a collector:** Any 100-pointer from this list. Latour 2010, Guigal La La's 2015, Chave 2015
- **Smart flex:** Pontet-Canet 2010 (100 RP) — often €150-200, punches with first growths

### Score Reliability Note
Scores are vintage-specific. A château scoring 100 in 2010 might score 89 in a weak year. Always verify the specific vintage. When in doubt, 2015, 2016, 2019, 2020 are strong across most French regions.
