# Burgundy (Bourgogne), France

## Overview

Burgundy is the reference for **Pinot Noir** (red) and **Chardonnay** (white). Wines are site-driven: **climat** = named vineyard; **domaine** = estate that grows and bottles; **négociant** = buys grapes or wine and bottles. Village and premier/grand cru names on the label indicate quality and style.

## White Burgundy (Chardonnay)

- **Chablis:** Northern Burgundy. Steely, mineral, citrus, oyster-friendly. Often unoaked or lightly oaked. **Grand cru** (e.g. Les Clos) = top; **premier cru** = step below. Best with seafood, raw fish, goat cheese.
- **Côte de Beaune:** Heart of white Burgundy. **Meursault:** Richer, nutty, buttery, stone fruit; great with poultry, lobster, cream sauces. **Puligny-Montrachet** and **Chassagne-Montrachet:** More tension and mineral; Puligny often finer and more linear; Chassagne can be rounder. Both pair with shellfish, roast chicken, truffle.
- **Mâconnais:** South of Beaune. **Pouilly-Fuissé, Saint-Véran, Mâcon-Villages:** Good value, fresh, apple and citrus; everyday white and seafood.

**When to recommend:** "Elegant white," "something like white Bordeaux but different," seafood, refined dinner, "best Chardonnay." Lead with village name (Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet, Chablis) then premier/grand cru if they want to spend more.

## Red Burgundy (Pinot Noir)

- **Côte de Nuits:** Power and depth. **Gevrey-Chambertin:** Structured, dark fruit, spice. **Vosne-Romanée:** Silky, floral, often the most sought-after. **Nuits-Saint-Georges:** Earthy, firm. **Chambolle-Musigny:** Lighter, fragrant. **Morey-Saint-Denis:** Between Gevrey and Chambolle in style.
- **Côte de Beaune (red):** **Beaune, Pommard, Volnay:** Softer than Nuits in general; Volnay often the most delicate. **Corton:** Can be powerful.
- **Beaujolais:** See beaujolais.md (Gamay, not Pinot; but often grouped with Burgundy for geography).

**When to recommend:** "Elegant red," "Pinot Noir," duck, lamb, mushroom dishes, romantic dinner. Lead with village (Gevrey-Chambertin, Vosne-Romanée, etc.) or "Côte de Nuits" / "Côte de Beaune."

## Key terms

- **Grand cru:** Top vineyard classification; highest level.
- **Premier cru (1er cru):** Second level; still excellent.
- **Village wine:** Named after the village (e.g. Meursault, Gevrey-Chambertin) without a specific vineyard; good entry point.
- **Domaine vs négociant:** Domaine = estate-grown; négociant = bought grapes/wine. Both can be excellent; domaines often emphasize terroir.

## Food pairings (summary)

- **Chablis:** Oysters, raw fish, goat cheese, light seafood.
- **Meursault / Puligny / Chassagne:** Lobster, poultry, cream sauces, truffle, richer fish.
- **Red Burgundy:** Duck, lamb, game, mushrooms, roast chicken.

## Producers to know

- **White:** Chablis — Raveneau, Dauvissat, William Fèvre. Meursault — Domaine des Comtes Lafon, Roulot, Coche-Dury, Arnaud Ente. Puligny/Chassagne — Leflaive, Domaine Leflaive, Henri Boillot, Marc Morey. Mâcon — Guffens-Heynen (Verget), Domaine Valette.
- **Red:** Gevrey-Chambertin — Armand Rousseau, Domaine Dujac, Bruno Clair. Vosne-Romanée — Domaine de la Romanée-Conti (DRC), Leroy, Méo-Camuzet, Cathiard. Chambolle — Comte de Vogüé, Barthod. Volnay — Lafarge, d'Angerville. Négociants: Louis Jadot, Faiveley, Bouchard Père.

## Vintage (short)

Strong recent vintages: 2015 (rich, structured), 2016 (fresh, balanced), 2019 (generous, approachable), 2020 (elegant, fine). Drink village and premier cru often 5–15 years; grand cru 10–25+ depending on site and vintage. For "drink now" suggest 2019 or 2020 village-level.

## Service

- **White:** 10–12 °C (Chablis cooler 8–10 °C; Meursault/Puligny 10–12 °C). Do not overchill; it mutes flavor.
- **Red:** 14–16 °C (Côte de Nuits can be 15–16 °C). Decant young, structured reds (e.g. Gevrey, Nuits) 30–45 min; old or delicate (e.g. Volnay, older Burgundy) decant briefly or not at all.

## Guest-led

- If they want **value in white Burgundy** → Mâcon, Saint-Véran, or Pouilly-Fuissé; if they want **the best** → Meursault or Puligny-Montrachet premier cru (name Lafon, Roulot, Leflaive when appropriate).
- If they want **mineral, no oak** → Chablis (village or premier cru); name Raveneau or Dauvissat if splurge.
- If they want **red for duck** → Volnay or Vosne-Romanée; silky tannin and red fruit with the fat.
- If they want **to impress with red** → Grand cru (Chambertin, Romanée-Conti, Musigny) or top premier cru; name DRC, Rousseau, Vogüé when appropriate.
