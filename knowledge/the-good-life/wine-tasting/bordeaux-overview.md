# Bordeaux, France — Overview

## Structure

Bordeaux is split by the Gironde estuary. **Left Bank:** Médoc (including Margaux, Saint-Julien, Pauillac, Saint-Estèphe), Graves, Pessac-Léognan. Cabernet Sauvignon–dominant; structured, tannic, age-worthy. **Right Bank:** Saint-Émilion, Pomerol, Fronsac. Merlot-dominant; rounder, earlier-drinking often. **Sweet white:** Sauternes and Barsac (see sauternes.md).

## Classifications

- **1855 Médoc (and Haut-Brion from Graves):** First to fifth growths (premiers crus to cinquièmes crus). Margaux, Lafite, Latour, Mouton, Haut-Brion are first growths. Use for "best Bordeaux," cellar, investment.
- **1959 Graves:** Pessac-Léognan (red and white); see pessac-leognan.md. Château Olivier, Domaine de Chevalier, Smith Haut Lafitte, etc.
- **Saint-Émilion:** Has its own classification (premier grand cru classé A and B, grand cru classé). **Cheval Blanc, Ausone, Figeac** among the top. Merlot/Cabernet Franc; silkier than Left Bank.
- **Pomerol:** No official classification; **Petrus, Le Pin, Lafleur** are among the most sought-after. Merlot-led; plush, opulent.

## When to recommend

- **Classic "Bordeaux" red, steak, cellar:** Left Bank (Margaux, Pauillac, Saint-Julien, Pessac-Léognan). See margaux.md, pessac-leognan.md.
- **Softer, Merlot-led, romantic dinner:** Right Bank (Saint-Émilion, Pomerol).
- **Bordeaux white:** Pessac-Léognan / Graves. See chateau-olivier.md, pessac-leognan.md, **bordeaux-dry-whites.md** (curated list: Pavillon blanc, Haut-Brion blanc, Domaine de Chevalier, Chantegrive Cuvée Caroline, Entre-Deux-Mers, etc.).
- **Second wines (seconds vins):** See **bordeaux-seconds-vins.md** (Pavillon Rouge, Petit Mouton, Pagodes de Cos, Bahans/Clarence Haut-Brion, Réserve de la Comtesse, Clémentin de Pape-Clément, N°3 d'Angelus, Goulée, etc.).
- **Grands vins rouges (curated by appellation):** See **bordeaux-reds-curated-list.md** (Saint-Émilion Grand Cru & classé, Pomerol, Fronsac, Montagne/Lussac/Puisseguin/Saint-Georges, Bordeaux Supérieur, Bourg/Cadillac/Castillon/Francs, Haut-Médoc, Margaux, Moulis, Pauillac, Saint-Estèphe, Saint-Julien, Graves, Pessac-Léognan).
- **Sweet wine, dessert, foie gras:** Sauternes. See sauternes.md.

## Key appellations (quick reference)

- **Margaux:** Elegant, perfumed; see margaux.md.
- **Pauillac:** Power and structure; Lafite, Latour, Mouton.
- **Saint-Julien:** Balance of power and finesse; Léoville, Ducru-Beaucaillou.
- **Saint-Estèphe:** Firmer, more tannic; good value.
- **Pessac-Léognan:** Red and white; see pessac-leognan.md, chateau-olivier.md.
- **Saint-Émilion:** Merlot-led; Cheval Blanc, Ausone, Figeac, Angelus.
- **Pomerol:** Merlot; Petrus, Le Pin, Vieux Château Certan.

## Producers to know (châteaux)

- **Left Bank (Médoc, Graves):** First growths — Château Margaux, Lafite Rothschild, Latour, Mouton Rothschild, Haut-Brion (Graves). Other — Léoville Las Cases, Ducru-Beaucaillou, Pichon Comtesse, Palmer, Pontet-Canet. Pessac-Léognan: see pessac-leognan.md (Olivier, Domaine de Chevalier, Smith Haut Lafitte).
- **Right Bank:** Saint-Émilion — Cheval Blanc, Ausone, Figeac, Angelus, Pavie. Pomerol — Petrus, Le Pin, Lafleur, Vieux Château Certan, L'Église-Clinet.
- **Sweet:** See sauternes.md (Yquem, Climens, Rieussec, Suduiraut).

## Vintage (short)

Strong recent vintages: 2015, 2016, 2019, 2020 (Left and Right Bank). Drink now: 2014, 2017, 2018 for earlier approachability. First growths and top Right Bank: often 15–30+ years. Decant young Left Bank 1–2 hours; Right Bank 30–60 min.

## Service

- **Red:** 16–18 °C. Decant young, tannic Left Bank (Margaux, Pauillac, Saint-Julien) 1–2 hours; Right Bank 30–60 min. Old bottles: decant briefly to separate sediment.
- **White (Pessac-Léognan):** 10–12 °C.

## Guest-led

- If they want **classic "Bordeaux" red, steak or lamb** → Left Bank (Margaux, Pauillac); name Lafite, Latour, or Palmer when appropriate.
- If they want **softer, Merlot-led, romantic** → Saint-Émilion (Cheval Blanc, Figeac) or Pomerol (Vieux Château Certan).
- If they want **to impress** → First growth or Cheval Blanc / Petrus tier; decant and serve at 17 °C.
- If they want **Bordeaux white** → Pessac-Léognan (Château Olivier, Domaine de Chevalier); mineral, citrus; 10–12 °C.
