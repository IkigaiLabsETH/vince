# Château Olivier (Pessac-Léognan, Bordeaux)

## Overview

Château Olivier is an historic estate in **Pessac-Léognan** (Graves), Bordeaux. It is one of six estates classified for **both red and white** in the 1959 Graves classification. The property has been producing wine for centuries and is known for a classic Graves profile in both colors.

## Château Olivier Blanc (White)

**Appellation:** Pessac-Léognan AOC (Graves, Bordeaux).

**Blend:** Typically Sémillon and Sauvignon Blanc—classic Graves white varieties.

**Profile:** Classic Graves white: citrus (lemon, grapefruit), white flowers, subtle waxy texture, and mineral notes. Balanced, with good acidity and structure. Develops with bottle age.

**Aging:** Can age 5–15+ years in good vintages; often approachable from 3–5 years.

**Food pairings:** Seafood (especially shellfish, grilled fish), poultry, goat cheese, asparagus, light cream sauces. A strong choice for a refined lunch or apéritif.

**Why it stands out:** One of the few classed-growth whites in Bordeaux. Serious, traditional, and consistently good—a clear recommendation when someone asks for a Pessac-Léognan or Graves white.
