# Armagnac — French Grape Brandy from Gascony

## Overview

**Armagnac** is the oldest French eau-de-vie; produced in **Gascony** (southwest France). **Single continuous distillation** (vs Cognac's double pot still) gives a **more aromatic, often more rustic and characterful** spirit. Styles range from delicate (Bas-Armagnac) to powerful (Ténarèze). Recommend for "French brandy but not Cognac," "after dinner with character," or "vintage brandy."

## AOC / terroir

- **Bas-Armagnac:** Sandy soils; **finest, most elegant**; fruity, floral; the reference for finesse.
- **Armagnac-Ténarèze:** Clay-limestone; **powerful, structured**; longer aging potential.
- **Haut-Armagnac:** Smaller production; lighter; less common on lists.

## Grapes

- **Ugni Blanc** (main), **Baco** (Armagnac-specific, aromatic), **Colombard**, **Folle Blanche**. Baco and Folle Blanche add perfume and structure.

## Age statements

- **VS:** Minimum 1 year in oak.
- **VSOP:** Minimum 4 years.
- **XO / Hors d'âge:** Minimum 10 years.
- **Vintage / millésime:** Single year; often bottled after long aging; **Darroze** is famous for single-vintage releases.

## Key producers

- **Darroze:** **Single-vintage (millésime)** releases; Bas-Armagnac and Ténarèze; reference for vintage Armagnac. "Look for a year; they release many vintages."
- **Château de Laubade:** Bas-Armagnac; estate-grown; VSOP, XO, vintages; **refined, consistent.**
- **Larressingle:** Bas-Armagnac; classic range; value and quality.
- **Delord:** Family house; XO, 25-year, vintages; **traditional.**
- **Gélas:** Fine Armagnac; older releases; **collector interest.**
- **Domaine d'Espérance:** Organic; Bas-Armagnac; **modern, clean.**
- **Château de Lacquy:** Estate; Bas-Armagnac; **artisanal.**

## Armagnac vs Cognac

- **Region:** Armagnac = Gascony; Cognac = Charente.
- **Distillation:** Armagnac = single continuous (column); Cognac = double pot still. Armagnac keeps more aroma and character; Cognac is often **rounder, more refined** and globally known.
- **Style:** Armagnac = **more aromatic, rustic, varied** (prune, fig, spice, rancio); Cognac = **smoother, nutty, vanilla.** Recommend Armagnac when the guest wants "something with more character" or "French brandy off the beaten path."

## Service

- **Glass:** Tulip (brandy snifter) or small wine glass; concentrate aromas.
- **Temperature:** Room temperature; **neat** or a **drop of water** to open.
- **When:** After dinner; with **coffee, dark chocolate**, or alone. Do not overchill.

## When to recommend / guest-led

- **"After dinner" / "something with character"** → Armagnac; name Darroze (vintage) or Laubade (XO); "French grape brandy from Gascony; more aromatic than Cognac."
- **"French brandy but not Cognac"** → Armagnac; "single distillation, Gascony; Darroze for vintages, Laubade or Larressingle for XO."
- **"Vintage Armagnac"** → Darroze single-vintage releases; "they bottle by year; pick a vintage or ask for a recommendation."
- **"Best Armagnac"** → Darroze (vintage or aged), Château de Laubade XO or vintage; "Bas-Armagnac for finesse, Ténarèze for power."
