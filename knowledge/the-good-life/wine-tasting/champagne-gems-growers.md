# Champagne Gems — The Connoisseur's Guide Beyond the Big Houses

The grandes marques (Krug, Dom Pérignon, Cristal, Bollinger) are the obvious plays. This file is about the bottles that make sommeliers and collectors lean in — grower Champagnes, single-vineyard cuvées, and the hidden gems that punch way above their price.

## Understanding Grower Champagne

**RM (Récoltant-Manipulant):** Grows own grapes, makes own wine. The terroir shows. Often small production (5,000-50,000 bottles vs millions for big houses).

**Why it matters:** Grower Champagne is to Champagne what Burgundy domaines are to Burgundy négociants — site-specific, artisanal, personal. Often 30-60% cheaper than equivalent-quality grande marque.

**How to spot:** Look for "RM" in tiny text on the label (bottom, near the registration number).

## THE GROWER ICONS (Cult Status)

### Jacques Selosse (Avize, Côte des Blancs)

- **The story:** Anselme Selosse revolutionized grower Champagne. Burgundian approach — barrel fermentation, lees aging, terroir expression. Tiny production. Waitlists.
- **Substance** — Perpetual reserve (solera). Oxidative, complex, nothing like "normal" Champagne. 97 JS. ~€200-300.
- **Version Originale (VO)** — NV, barrel-fermented. Intense, mineral. ~€150.
- **Initial** — Entry cuvée. Still extraordinary. ~€90-120.
- **Le Mesnil Les Carelles** — Single-site, blanc de blancs. Precise, mineral. ~€250+.
- **Vintage bottlings** (2002, 2003) — Landmark. €500+.
- **Kelly tip:** If you find Selosse, buy it. Allocation only. Don't decant — just open and wait 20 minutes in the glass.

### Egly-Ouriet (Ambonnay, Montagne de Reims)

- **The story:** Francis Egly. Grand Cru Ambonnay (Pinot Noir heartland). Low yields, late disgorgement, minimal dosage. Power and precision.
- **Grand Cru Brut Tradition** — NV but aged 4+ years on lees. Vinous, powerful. 96 JS. ~€60-80. ABSURD value.
- **Blanc de Noirs Grand Cru** — 100% Pinot Noir. Rich, structured, gastronomic. ~€80-100.
- **Brut Millésimé** (various years) — Grand Cru vintage. 95-97 pts. ~€100-150.
- **Les Vignes de Vrigny** — Premier Cru, Pinot Meunier. Shows what Meunier can do in great hands. ~€50-60.
- **Kelly tip:** Egly-Ouriet Tradition is the single best value in serious Champagne. Period.

### Pierre Péters (Le Mesnil-sur-Oger, Côte des Blancs)

- **The story:** Rodolphe Péters. Grand Cru blanc de blancs specialist. Mineral, precise, terroir-driven.
- **Cuvée de Réserve** — NV blanc de blancs. Pure Chardonnay, Le Mesnil minerality. ~€40-50. 92-93 JS.
- **Extra Brut Grand Cru** — Zero-dosage. Taut, mineral. ~€45-55.
- **Les Chétillons** — Single old-vine parcel in Le Mesnil. The crown jewel. 95-97 pts. ~€80-120.
- **Héritage** — Prestige cuvée. ~€100+.
- **Kelly tip:** Péters Cuvée de Réserve at €40 is the smartest blanc de blancs in Champagne.

### Larmandier-Bernier (Vertus, Côte des Blancs)

- **The story:** Pierre Larmandier. Biodynamic pioneer in Champagne. Chardonnay-focused, minimal intervention.
- **Terre de Vertus** — Non-dosé, Premier Cru. Pure, chalky. Vintage (2008 = 96 JD). ~€50-70.
- **Longitude** — NV Extra Brut. Blanc de blancs, precise. ~€35-45.
- **Vieille Vigne du Levant** — Grand Cru, old vines. Complex, age-worthy. ~€80-120.
- **Kelly tip:** Terre de Vertus 2008 is one of the greatest Champagnes of the decade under €70.

### Agrapart (Avize, Côte des Blancs)

- **The story:** Pascal Agrapart. Grand Cru Chardonnay (Avize, Oger, Cramant). Organic/biodynamic.
- **Les 7 Crus** — NV brut. Blend of Grand Cru sites. ~€40-50. 92-93.
- **Complantée** — Field blend of all 7 Champagne grapes (!). Unique. ~€60-80.
- **Avizoise** — Extra Brut, Grand Cru Avize. 95-96. ~€70-90.
- **Vénus** — Blanc de blancs from a single old-vine parcel. Prestige. ~€120+.
- **Kelly tip:** Complantée is the conversation starter — 7 grapes in one Champagne. Nobody else does this.

## THE RISING STARS

### Vouette & Sorbée (Aube)

- Bertrand Gautherot. Biodynamic, minimal sulfur. Textural, wild. **Fidèle** (NV brut, Pinot Noir) — ~€35-45. **Saignée de Sorbée** (rosé) — stunning. 91-93.

### Ulysse Collin (Congy, Côte de Sézanne)

- Olivier Collin. Ex-corporate turned vigneron. Single-parcel focus. **Les Maillons** (Pinot Noir), **Les Pierrieres** (Chardonnay), **Les Roises** (Chardonnay). All extra brut, oak-aged. 94-96. ~€80-120. Extremely limited.

### Cédric Bouchard / Roses de Jeanne (Aube)

- Single-vineyard, single-vintage, single-grape. Each cuvée = one parcel. **La Bolorée** (Blanc de blancs), **Les Ursules** (Blanc de noirs). 93-96. ~€60-90. Tiny production (1,000-3,000 bottles per cuvée).

### Jérôme Prévost / La Closerie (Gueux)

- Single vineyard **Les Béguines** — 100% Pinot Meunier. The wine that proved Meunier is a noble grape. Extra brut. 95-96 JS. ~€80-120.

### Emmanuel Brochet (Villers-aux-Nœuds, Montagne de Reims)

- **Le Mont Benoît** — Single vineyard, Extra Brut. Organic. Precise, mineral. 94-95. ~€60-80.

### Laherte Frères (Chavot-Courcourt, Vallée de la Marne)

- **Les 7** — Multi-vintage, 7 parcels. ~€35-40. 91-92. Great entry.
- **Les Vignes d'Autrefois** — Old-vine Meunier. ~€50-60. 93-94.

### Bérêche & Fils (Ludes, Montagne de Reims)

- **Brut Réserve** — NV, 3 cépages. ~€35-40. 91-92. Transparent, terroir-driven.
- **Le Cran** — Premier Cru, vintage. 94-96. ~€80-100.

## THE BIG HOUSE HIDDEN GEMS (Overlooked Cuvées)

### Charles Heidsieck Brut Réserve — €49

The insiders' NV. 60% reserve wines (aged 10+ years in chalk cellars). Brioche, exotic fruit, long finish. 93 JS. Per TGL: "redevenu le dandy qu'il n'aurait jamais dû cesser d'être." The best-kept secret among grandes marques.

### Champagne Lallier Ouvrage Grand Cru Extra Brut — €148

TGL's Christmas 2025 Champagne pick. Grand Cru, extra brut. Serious, gastronomic.

### Pol Roger Sir Winston Churchill — ~€200

The prestige cuvée nobody talks about (everyone's busy with DP and Cristal). Pinot-dominant, structured, built to age. Churchill's personal Champagne. 96-97 JS.

### Taittinger Comtes de Champagne Blanc de Blancs — ~€150-180

Pure Grand Cru Chardonnay. 2012 = 97 JS. One of the most elegant prestige cuvées. Less flashy than Cristal, more refined.

### Ruinart Dom Ruinart Blanc de Blancs — ~€150-200

The oldest Champagne house's prestige. Pure Chardonnay. 2010 = 96 JS. Mineral, taut, ages beautifully.

### Salon — ~€400-600

One wine. One grape (Chardonnay). One village (Le Mesnil). Only made in exceptional years (~37 vintages since 1905). 2012 = 99 JS. The most exclusive Champagne that isn't marketing.

### Philipponnat Clos des Goisses — ~€150-250

Single-vineyard monopole. The steepest vineyard in Champagne (45° slope). South-facing, on the Marne. Powerful, structured, ages decades. 95-97 JS. The sommelier's flex pick.
