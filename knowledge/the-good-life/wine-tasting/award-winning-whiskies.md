# Award-Winning Whiskies — Deep Knowledge

Use this note so Kelly can recommend the **very best** whiskies with confidence: top bottles, major awards, and when to suggest each. For **current-year** winners (e.g. latest World Whiskies Awards or Jim Murray's Whisky Bible), use **WEB_SEARCH** and say you looked it up. This file covers perennial award winners and historically top-scoring expressions.

---

## Major awards (what they mean)

- **World Whiskies Awards (WWA):** Annual; categories by region and style (World's Best Single Malt, World's Best Bourbon, World's Best Japanese Single Malt, etc.). Blind-tasted; highly respected. Use WEB_SEARCH for the latest year.
- **San Francisco World Spirits Competition (SFWSC):** Double Gold = every judge gave Gold; Best in Show per category. Premier US competition; use WEB_SEARCH for current Double Gold / Best in Show.
- **IWSC (International Wine & Spirit Competition):** Global; Gold, Silver, Bronze; "Best in Class" and trophy winners. Use WEB_SEARCH for current results.
- **Jim Murray's Whisky Bible:** Annual scores and "World Whisky of the Year"; influential but controversial—scores change every year. For current "World Whisky of the Year" or specific scores, use WEB_SEARCH.

## Scotch single malt — award-tier bottles

### Speyside (fruit, honey, often unpeated; accessible yet premium)

- **The Macallan** — Sherry Oak 12/18/25, Double Cask, Fine & Rare series. Consistently tops luxury and single malt categories; Sherry cask is the reference. "Rich, dried fruit, spice, long finish; gift and prestige."
- **GlenAllachie** — 12 Year Old (WWA World's Best Single Malt 2025), 15, 18, 21; Billy Walker–led; rich, sultana, chocolate, spice. "Award-winning Speyside; rich and oily."
- **GlenDronach** — 12/15/18/21, Revival, Parliament 21; Sherry-led; dried fruit, toffee, Christmas cake. Perennial WWA and SFWSC category winners.
- **Glenfarclas** — 15/21/25/30/40, Family Cask; family-owned; Sherry, honey, nuts. "Classic Speyside; value at 15 and 21."
- **Glenfiddich** — 12/15/18/21, Grand Cru, Excellence; widely available; pear, apple, honey. 18 and 21 often in award lists.
- **The Glenlivet** — 12/15/18/21/25, Founder's Reserve, Illicit Still; apple, citrus, floral. Core range and 21+ for gifting.
- **Balvenie** — DoubleWood 12/17, Caribbean Cask 14, Tun 1509, 21 PortWood; honey, vanilla, subtle spice. DoubleWood and Caribbean Cask frequently win Gold/Double Gold.
- **Glenrothes** — 12/18/25; berry, orange, vanilla. Glenrothes 18 has taken SFWSC Double Gold (e.g. 2024).
- **Aberlour** — 12/16/18, A'bunadh (cask strength Sherry); rich Sherry, dried fruit. A'bunadh is a benchmark cask-strength Sherry.
- **Strathisla** — 12/18; Chivas backbone; honey, malt, fruit. Under-the-radar Speyside.

### Islay (peat, smoke, brine; "peated" and "smoky" requests)

- **Ardbeg** — 10, Uigeadail, Corryvreckan, An Oa, 19 Traigh Bhan, 25. Uigeadail and Corryvreckan regularly win World's Best Single Malt or category. "Brine, smoke, medicinal; long finish; for peat lovers."
- **Laphroaig** — 10, 10 Cask Strength, Lore, 25, 32. 10 and Lore often in WWA/SFWSC. "Classic Islay; iodine, smoke, seaweed."
- **Lagavulin** — 8/11/12/16, 12 Year Old Cask Strength, 26. 16 is the iconic Islay; 12 CS and special releases win awards. "Smoke, pepper, dried fruit; long finish."
- **Bruichladdich** — Classic Laddie (unpeated), Port Charlotte (peated), Octomore (super-heavily peated), Black Art. Octomore and Black Art frequently win World's Best or category. "Progressive Islay; Octomore for maximum peat."
- **Bunnahabhain** — 12/18/25, Toiteach (peated), Stiuireadair. 18 often in Gold lists; unpeated style is nutty, coastal.
- **Kilchoman** — Machir Bay, Sanaig, Loch Gorm, 100% Islay. Farm distillery; WWA/SFWSC category winners. "Young, vibrant peat; citrus and smoke."
- **Caol Ila** — 12/18/25, Unpeated, Moch. Diageo workhorse; 12 and 18 are consistent; "gentler Islay; smoke and citrus."

### Highland (varied; coastal to fruity)

- **Glenmorangie** — 10, Lasanta, Quinta Ruban, Nectar d'Or, Signet. **Signet** has won World's Best Single Malt (WWA) and is a prestige recommendation; "chocolate, coffee, orange; no age statement, exceptional."
- **Highland Park** — 12/18/21/25/30, Cask Strength, Dragon Legend. 18 and 25 regularly in "best" lists; "heather, honey, light smoke; balanced."
- **Dalmore** — 12/15/18, King Alexander III, Cigar Malt, 25/35. King Alexander and Cigar Malt are award-tier; "orange, chocolate, Sherry."
- **Oban** — 14/18, Little Bay, Distiller's Edition. 14 is a classic; "fruit, salt, light smoke; West Highland."
- **Glengoyne** — 10/12/18/21/25/30; unpeated; apple, toffee, Sherry. 21 and 25 often Gold/Double Gold. "Slow distillation; rich and fruity."

### Lowland (lighter, floral)

- **Glenkinchie** — 12, Distiller's Edition; grass, citrus, cereal. "Classic Lowland; light and approachable."
- **Auchentoshan** — 12/18/21, Three Wood; triple-distilled; citrus, vanilla, nutty. "Lowland triple distillation; smooth."

### Campbeltown (oily, maritime, characterful)

- **Springbank** — 10/15/18/21/25, Longrow (peated), Hazelburn (unpeated). 10 and 15 regularly win World's Best or category. "Oily, brine, fruit; handcrafted; allocation-dependent."
- **Glen Scotia** — 15/18/25, Victoriana, Double Cask. 15 and Victoriana often in WWA/SFWSC. "Maritime, honey, spice; Campbeltown revival."

### Blended Scotch (prestige)

- **Johnnie Walker** — Blue Label, Blue Label Ghost & Rare, 18; consistent luxury blend. "Smooth, complex; gift and after-dinner."
- **Chivas Regal** — 18/25; honey, apple, smooth. 18 often in award rounds.
- **Compass Box** — Hedonism, The Spaniard, The Story of the Spaniard, Flaming Heart; innovative; often WWA/SFWSC. "Designer blends; Sherry and spice."

## Irish whiskey — award-tier bottles

- **Redbreast** — 12/15/21/27; single pot still; "World's Best Pot Still" and "Irish Whiskey of the Year" multiple times. 12 and 15 are core; 21 and 27 for prestige. "Dried fruit, spice, oily; the reference Irish."
- **Green Spot** / **Yellow Spot** / **Blue Spot** — Single pot still; Green (7yr), Yellow (12yr), Blue (7yr cask strength). Green and Yellow often Gold; Blue for power. "Fruit, malt, vanilla; Mitchell & Son."
- **Midleton Very Rare** — Annual release; blend of pot still, malt, grain. "Irish luxury blend; honey, pepper, orchard fruit."
- **Jameson** — 18 Year Old, Crested, Black Barrel; 18 and Crested in award lists. "Smooth, accessible; 18 for gifting."
- **Bushmills** — 16/21 Single Malt; triple-distilled; honey, malt. 21 has won World's Best Irish Single Malt.
- **Teeling** — Single Malt, Single Pot Still, Brabazon, Blackpitts (peated). Single Malt and Single Pot Still often WWA/SFWSC. "Dublin distillery; modern Irish."
- **Connemara** — Peated single malt; "smoky Irish; peat and honey."

## American whiskey — award-tier bottles

### Bourbon (sweet, vanilla, caramel; Kentucky heartland)

- **Buffalo Trace** — Benchmark; Weller (Wheated), Eagle Rare, Stagg Jr., George T. Stagg, Van Winkle (Pappy) from same group. "Vanilla, caramel, oak; entry to prestige."
- **Eagle Rare** — 10 Year, 17 Year (BTAC); "single barrel" at 10; cherry, oak, vanilla. 17 often Best Bourbon contender.
- **Four Roses** — Single Barrel, Small Batch, Small Batch Select; Limited Edition (LE) annual. LE and Single Barrel frequently Double Gold / Best Bourbon. "Floral, fruit, spice; ten recipes."
- **Woodford Reserve** — Double Oaked, Master's Collection; Double Oaked often SFWSC Double Gold. "Rich, chocolate, vanilla; double-barrel finish."
- **Maker's Mark** — 46, Private Select, limited editions; wheated. "Wheat softness; caramel, vanilla."
- **Knob Creek** — 9/12/15 Year, 12 Year often in "best" lists. "Full-bodied; peanut, oak, spice."
- **Old Forester** — 1920 Prohibition Style, Birthday Bourbon, Statesman; 1920 and Birthday often award winners. "Banana, oak, spice."
- **Wild Turkey** — Russell's Reserve 10/13, Master's Keep series; 13 and Master's Keep in prestige lists. "Bold; spice, caramel, oak."
- **Elijah Craig** — Small Batch, Barrel Proof (ECBP), 18/23 Year; ECBP batches frequently SFWSC Double Gold (e.g. C923). "Rich, oak, caramel; cask strength for power."
- **Michter's** — US\*1, 10 Year, 20 Year, 25 Year; 10 and 20 often Best Bourbon or category. "Elegant; toffee, fruit, spice."
- **Heaven Hill** — Elijah Craig, Larceny, William Heavenhill, Parker's Heritage; ECBP and Parker's in award rounds. "Classic Kentucky; value and prestige."
- **Pappy Van Winkle** — 15/20/23 Year (wheated); allocated, prestige. "Ultimate gift bourbon; caramel, oak, dried fruit; note availability."
- **Weller** — 12, Full Proof, CYPB, Single Barrel; wheated Buffalo Trace; allocated. "Wheated Pappy family; smooth, sweet."
- **Bardstown** — Discovery, Fusion, Collaborative; Discovery series often Double Gold. "Modern Kentucky; layered, complex."
- **New Riff** — Bottled in Bond, Single Barrel; high-rye; "young but award-winning; spice, fruit."

### Rye (spicier; cocktails and sipping)

- **WhistlePig** — 10/12/15/18, Boss Hog, Farm Stock; 10 and 12 often WWA/SFWSC. "Vermont rye; dill, spice, citrus; 15 and Boss Hog for prestige."
- **Sazerac Rye** — 18 Year (BTAC); "World's Best Rye" contender. "Mint, spice, oak; allocated."
- **Van Winkle Family Reserve Rye** — 13 Year; allocated. "Rye for collectors; smooth, spice."
- **Pikesville** — 6 Year 110 proof; Heaven Hill; "spice, cherry, oak; high proof."
- **Willett** — 4 Year Rye, Family Estate Rye; "purple top"; often Double Gold. "Orange, mint, spice; Kentucky rye."
- **High West** — Rendezvous Rye, A Midwinter Night's Dram, Double Rye; Rendezvous and Midwinter in award lists. "Utah; finished rye; dried fruit, spice."

## Japanese whisky — award-tier bottles

- **Yamazaki** — 12/18/25, Mizunara, Sherry Cask, Distiller's Reserve. 12 and 18 frequently World's Best or category; Sherry Cask 2013 was Murray's World Whisky of the Year. "Refined, Mizunara oak, dried fruit; Suntory flagship."
- **Hakushu** — 12/18/25, Distiller's Reserve; "Japanese forest"; light smoke, citrus, herb. 12 and 18 in award rounds.
- **Hibiki** — Harmony, 17/21/30; blend; Harmony widely available; 21 and 30 prestige. "Blend; floral, honey, orange; gift staple."
- **Nikka** — Yoichi (peated), Miyagikyo (fruity), From the Barrel, Taketsuru (blend), Single Cask. Yoichi and Miyagikyo single malts often WWA/SFWSC; From the Barrel for value. "Yoichi: smoke and fruit; Miyagikyo: delicate, fruity."
- **Mars** — Tsunuki (WWA Best Japanese Single Malt 2025), Iwai, Komagatake; Tsunuki 2022/2024 editions have taken Double Gold and WWA. "Tsunuki: dried leafy, broth, caramel, smoke; award-winning."
- **Kanosuke** — Single Malt 2022 LE and others; SFWSC Double Gold (e.g. 2023). "Southern Japan; coastal, fruity."
- **Chichibu** — The Floor Malted, Ichiro's Malt, single casks; small batch; "World's Best Craft Whisky" contender. "Japanese craft; complex, layered."
- **Ichiro's Malt** — Double Distilleries, Card series (e.g. King of Diamonds); Chichibu/Mars. "Blended malt; rare; collector tier."
- **Karuizawa** — Discontinued; single casks; auction and collector. "Legendary; mention only for 'unicorn' or collector."

For **current-year** Japanese winners (e.g. Mars Tsunuki 2024, Kanosuke, new Nikka releases), use **WEB_SEARCH** and say you looked it up; availability and awards change yearly.

## World whisky (Taiwan, India, Australia, etc.)

- **Kavalan** — Taiwan; Soloist (Sherry/Vin Barrique/Port), Concertmaster, King Car; "World's Best Single Malt" (WWA) and multiple Double Gold. "Tropical aging; rich, fruity, bold; Solist Sherry is reference."
- **Amrut** — India; Fusion, Greedy Angels, Spectrum, single casks; WWA and SFWSC category winners. "Indian single malt; tropical; Fusion and Spectrum for complexity."
- **Paul John** — India; Brilliance, Edited (peated), Bold, Select Cask; often Gold/Double Gold. "Goa distillery; spice, fruit, peat in Edited."
- **Starward** — Australia; Nova, Fortis, Left Field; wine cask–led; WWA/SFWSC. "Australian; red wine casks; cherry, spice."
- **Sullivan's Cove** — Australia; American Oak, French Oak, Double Cask; "World's Best Single Malt" (WWA) past winner. "Tasmanian; elegant, fruity."
- **Lark** — Australia; Classic, Cask Strength, limited editions; "Tasmanian; peat and fruit."
- **Mackmyra** — Sweden; Brukswhisky, Moment, limited editions; "Nordic; juniper, apple, light smoke."
- **Penderyn** — Wales; Madeira, Sherry, Peated; "Welsh single malt; honey, fruit."

## Service (same as whiskey.md)

- **Glass:** Tulip (Glencairn-style) or tumbler for neat; preserves aroma.
- **Water:** A drop or small splash **opens** many whiskies; recommend "neat or with a drop of water."
- **Ice:** Optional; "neat or with a single cube" if guest likes chilled; ice dulls aroma over time.
- **Temperature:** Room temperature; do not heat.

## When to recommend (guest-led)

- **"After dinner" / "something not wine"** → Ask **peaty, smooth, or bourbon?** Then: Scotch (Ardbeg/Laphroaig for peaty, Macallan/GlenDronach for smooth), bourbon (Buffalo Trace, Four Roses, Woodford Double Oaked), or Irish (Redbreast 12/15).
- **"Peaty" / "smoky"** → Islay: Ardbeg Uigeadail, Laphroaig 10/Lore, Lagavulin 16, Octomore; or Springbank/Longrow; "brine, smoke, long finish."
- **"Smooth" / "easy"** → Speyside (Glenfiddich 18, GlenDronach 15, GlenAllachie 12), Irish (Redbreast 12, Green Spot), bourbon (Buffalo Trace, Maker's 46).
- **"Best single malt" / "award winner"** → GlenAllachie 12 (WWA World's Best Single Malt 2025), Glenmorangie Signet, Ardbeg Uigeadail, Springbank 10/15, GlenDronach 15/18; name one from this note; for **current** year use WEB_SEARCH.
- **"Best bourbon"** → Elijah Craig Barrel Proof, Four Roses LE/Single Barrel, Woodford Double Oaked, Eagle Rare 17; for prestige Pappy/Weller—note allocation; for current Double Gold use WEB_SEARCH.
- **"Best Japanese"** → Yamazaki 12/18, Hibiki Harmony/21, Nikka Yoichi/Miyagikyo, Mars Tsunuki; for current WWA/SFWSC Japanese winner use WEB_SEARCH.
- **"Best Irish"** → Redbreast 12/15/21, Green Spot, Yellow Spot, Midleton Very Rare.
- **"Gift" / "impress"** → Macallan 18 Sherry, Glenmorangie Signet, Redbreast 21, Yamazaki 18, Hibiki 21, Pappy 15/20 (if available), Kavalan Solist Sherry; name one and add one sentence tasting + service.
- **"Something different" / "world whisky"** → Kavalan Solist, Amrut Fusion, Paul John Edited, Starward Nova, Sullivan's Cove.

Always give **one clear pick** + one sentence tasting (profile, finish) + **one alternative**; add service (neat, drop of water) when relevant. For **current-year** awards (WWA, SFWSC, IWSC, Murray's Bible) use **WEB_SEARCH** and say you looked it up.
