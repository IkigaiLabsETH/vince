# The Good Life — Bordeaux & Biarritz/Pays Basque

Fine dining and high-end hotels sourced exclusively from The Good Life magazine (thegoodlife.fr).

---

## BORDEAUX

### Hotels

1. **Burdigala by Inwood Hotels** — 5★. Quarter Mériadeck, near city center. Recently renovated. Rooftop terrace for sunbathing. Private cinema room (can be privatized — kids' cartoons at breakfast, classic films year-round). Duplex suites on two levels (apartment feel) with jacuzzi bathtubs. Marshall speakers in rooms. Big glass atrium lobby.

2. **FirstName Bordeaux** — 4★ (JDV by Hyatt). 147 rooms + penthouse suite. 1980s concrete building in Mériadeck redesigned by architect Nicolas Adnet. Lifestyle/eco concept by Alboran Hotels group. No minibar — kitchenette on each floor instead. Digitalized check-in/out. Local Bordeaux producers throughout. Restaurant + buzzy bar. Staff work 4-day weeks. Not 5-star but notable for its design-forward approach. firstname.com.

3. **Maison Hubert** — Boutique maison d'hôtes, NOT a hotel. 5 suites (70–100m² each), 580m² total. Art Deco building on Rue Saint-Hubert, near Jardin Public/Chartrons. By Hobo Club (also behind Maison Fernand, Bistrot Maurice). Designed by B2P Architecture + Lola Pegoraro. Each suite = micro-universe (Suite Biggy Blush with pink bow-window, Maxi Sun with tropézienne terrace). Two bedrooms per suite — great for families. Locavore breakfast: Gondolo jams, Baratte Bordelaise butter, La Brûlerie coffee. Charlie Crane baby furniture, Yoyo strollers. Not 5-star but the most design-interesting stay in Bordeaux per TGL. maisonhubert.co. 1 Rue Saint-Hubert, 33000.

### Fine Dining

4. **Ressources** — 1★ Michelin. Chef Tanguy Laviale (ex-Garopapilles), sommelier Maxime Courvoisier. Open Mon–Fri dinner only. Single-price format: compose 3, 4, 5 courses or "no limit." 7 dishes on menu + 7 secret backup dishes for dietary needs. RAP US plays in the background. 700+ wine references, heavy on natural/emerging vignerons. Order: tempura de laitue de mer, gnocchi verts ail des ours + brebis, Montbéliarde maturée 3 weeks with kale/anchois/noix, Nicolas Berger chocolate cream + mushroom ice cream. Second address "Vivants" just opened — same precision, shared cellar, more casual. 126 Rue Fondaudège. Tel: 09 70 66 72 32.

5. **Bar Casa Bordeaux** — MOF Barman cocktail bar inside Grand Hôtel Français. Cocktails by Jérémy Lauilhé (Meilleur Ouvrier de France Barman 2023). Studio Crème design. Try: Umelixir (saké/umeshu/white pepper/sesame oil), Calice 2.0 (vermouth/aquavit/pélargonium), Pineapple Paloma (smoky-exotic). 6 signatures + 4 revisited classics. Open daily 18h–00h30. 12 Rue du Temple. Tel: 05 56 48 10 35.

6. **Horace Café** — Specialty coffee + brunch/goûter. Fresh, local, seasonal. Only 2 mains daily (one carnivore, one veggie), rotating weekly. Famous carrot cake. 40 Rue Poquelin Molière. Tel: 05 56 90 01 93.

7. **Galerie Doda** — Not a restaurant but a design gallery specializing in unreedited lighting from the Trente Glorieuses (1945–1975). Owner Thibaut Renesson Philipon also does in-home lighting consulting. 72 Rue Judaïque. Tel: 05 56 12 32 79.

8. **pH7** — Concept-store in Chartrons (Rue Notre-Dame, antiques row). "Wabi-sabi refuge." Bordeaux ceramics, Marie Michielsen plates, Isabelle Yamamoto hemp cushions, Senoufo stools, Chinese pottery, Nepalese wood jars. ph7bordeaux.com. 20 Rue Notre-Dame.

## BIARRITZ & PAYS BASQUE

### Hotels

9. **Hôtel du Palais Biarritz** — Palace (5★+), The Unbound Collection by Hyatt. The ONLY palace on the Atlantic coast. Napoleon III era, €65M renovation by Atelier Cos (same firm: Ritz Paris, Cheval Blanc Courchevel, Château de Saran). 142 rooms and suites, all rebuilt — some bathrooms moved to window-side. 1 Avenue de l'Impératrice. Tel: +33 5 59 41 12 34. hyatt.com.

10. **Brindos, Lac & Château** — Relais & Châteaux, Anglet (next to Biarritz). Called "the Château Marmont of Pays Basque" by TGL. Gothic ceilings, crystal chandeliers, Persian rugs. 29 rooms/suites + 11 floating bungalows on the lake (eco-self-sufficient, each with Nordic bath on terrace). Spa on a floating pontoon. Chef Hugo de la Barrière — locavore Basque cuisine in panoramic lakeside dining room. Exceptional cocktail bar: every cocktail has a NoLo (non-alcoholic) version — not watered-down, fully translated. Former Serge Blanco property, now BMF group. 1 Allée du Château, 64600 Anglet. brindos-cotebasque.com.

11. **La Réserve** — Saint-Jean-de-Luz. Between sea and town. 41 rooms (6 suites) in a grand Basque villa + 44 apartments in Résidence. Sports-focused: tennis, paddle, surf, deep-sea fishing, golf. Restaurant Ilura, chef Pierre Boffo. Pointe Saint-Barbe, 1 Avenue Gaëtan Bernoville. Tel: +33 5 59 51 32 00. hotel-lareserve.com.

12. **Le Grand Hôtel** — 5★, Saint-Jean-de-Luz. Fleuron of the bay. Built early 20th century, renovated 2015 (earned 5th star). Major spa under brick vaults: thalasso cures 3 or 5 days. Art Deco rooms with fuchsia/turquoise accents. 2 restaurants + bar. 43 Boulevard Thiers. Tel: +33 5 59 26 35 36. luzgrandhotel.fr.

13. **La Co(o)rniche** — Pyla-sur-Mer (technically Arcachon basin, ~2h from Biarritz but 1h from Bordeaux). By Philippe Starck. Infinity pool suspended between sky and sea atop Europe's highest dune. 11 rooms/suites + 18 wooden cabins. Former 1930s hunting lodge turned 80s party spot, now design hotel. Restaurant with Bassin d'Arcachon oysters. 46 Avenue Louis-Gaume. Tel: +33 5 56 22 72 11. lacoorniche-pyla.com.

### Fine Dining & Notable Tables

14. **Petit Grill Basque** — Saint-Jean-de-Luz. Chef Iñaki Aizpitarte (ex-Chateaubriand, Le Dauphin Paris — godfather of bistronomie). Took over a historic Luzien institution, kept the original hand-cranked ceiling fans and Louis Floutier frescoes. Cuisine: tripes de morue, veau rosé au beurre pimenté, riz aux carabineros. Bilingual Basque-French menu. Pair with Irouléguy nature. Next door: Chez Maya (his partner Delphine Zampetti's boutique-traiteur). 4 Rue Saint-Jacques, Saint-Jean-de-Luz.

15. **L'Impertinent** — Biarritz. Chef Fabian Feldmann. 1★ Michelin + 1 Grappe Michelin. (His second address Basa is in Bayonne — see below.)

16. **Basa** — Bayonne. By chef Fabian Feldmann (L'Impertinent, Biarritz). In former Tribunal de Commerce redesigned by architect Delphine Carrère. 95% sourced within 150km, organic. Pintxos, Bayonne ham, local Arto Gorria corn, plus miso/yuzu/bao/phở fusions. Two-story restaurant + terrace. 74 Rue d'Espagne, Bayonne. brasserie-basa.com.

17. **Harakina** — Bayonne. 42 Rue Port Neuf. Cantine vibes. Essential apéro from 19h — panko croquetas are the move. Franco-Basco-Spanish plates + natural wines.

## KELLY'S CONCIERGE NOTES

### Power Picks

- **Bordeaux splurge dinner:** Ressources (1★) — book Mon–Fri, go "no limit" carte blanche, let Maxime pick the wines
- **Bordeaux stay:** Burdigala 5★ for full-service; Maison Hubert for design lovers traveling with family
- **Biarritz palace:** Hôtel du Palais — the only Atlantic palace, period. Post-renovation it's world-class
- **Biarritz romantic:** Brindos floating lodge — Nordic bath on your private terrace, lake view, NoLo cocktails at the bar
- **Basque fine dining:** Petit Grill Basque (Aizpitarte) in Saint-Jean-de-Luz for the most exciting table in the region
- **Day trip from Hossegor:** Saint-Jean-de-Luz ~30min, Biarritz ~40min, Bayonne ~45min, Bordeaux ~1h45

### Booking Intel

- Ressources: dinner only Mon–Fri, book 1–2 weeks ahead
- Hôtel du Palais: book 2–3 months ahead for summer; rates spike July–August
- Brindos floating lodges: limited to 10 — book 1 month+ ahead for weekends
- Le Grand Hôtel SJL: thalasso cures need advance booking
- Petit Grill Basque: small room, no-reservation walk-in risky — call ahead
- La Co(o)rniche: summer weekends sell out fast, midweek is the move
