Fibonacci extensions are a popular technical tool in Bitcoin analysis, used to project potential cycle tops by extending ratios (commonly 1.618, 2.272, 2.618, 3.618, etc.) from a major swing low to high, then beyond the high. Traders draw these on logarithmic charts to account for BTC’s exponential growth, often from cycle bear market bottoms to previous highs.

Historical Accuracy

## Context

Fib extensions have remarkably aligned with past BTC cycle tops:

- 
2013 cycle — Topped near higher extensions (e.g., around 4.236-5.618 levels in some models).

- 
2017 cycle — ~$20,000 peak near the 2.272 Fib extension.

## Main

- 
2021 cycle — ~$69,000 high near the 1.618 Fib extension (diminishing returns as market matures).

Analysts note “decreasing multiples” over cycles due to BTC’s growing market cap and adoption (e.g., Golden Ratio Multiplier using 350DMA multiples).

Current Cycle (2024-2026) Predictions

As of late 2025 (BTC ~$88k post-correction from $126k ATH), common Fib-based targets for the blow-off top:

- 
Conservative/Base — $140k-$175k (1.618 extension, aligning with 2021’s level).

- 
Aggressive — $200k-$220k (higher extensions if euphoria extends).

- 
Extreme/Super Cycle — $300k+ (2.618-3.618 if no macro breakdown).

Timing often points to Q4 2025-Q1 2026 peak, followed by bear market.

These are self-fulfilling (many traders watch them) but not guarantees—macro events can override. Useful for setting profit targets, like our $150k sell plan. Classic tool with eerie historical hits—worth monitoring on log charts!
