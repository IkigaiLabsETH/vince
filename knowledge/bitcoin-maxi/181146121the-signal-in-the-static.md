The rest of the market follows in disciplined lockstep: Ethereum, Solana, the altcoin firmament, all tracing the same modest retreat. This is not panic. This is reverence. The entire cycle now leans forward to hear what kind of liquidity winter Jerome Powell intends to impose.

We have seen these pauses before. They are the moments when the weak hands are shaken out and the strong hands load the boat in silence. Europe spent November selling Bitcoin to pay its tax collectors; Standard Chartered trimmed its year-end prophecy from moonshot to a mere one hundred thousand. Such gestures are ceremonial. They change nothing about the underlying arithmetic: every cycle since 2009 has ended higher than the last, and this one is nowhere near its end.

## Main

In every capital, the same lesson is being taught at scale: fiat is only as strong as the geography it controls and the violence it can credibly threaten. When rivers become bargaining chips and two trillion dollars of foreign-owned bonds become a negotiating tactic, the social contract reveals its final form: coercion dressed in legal tender.

Bitcoin was built for exactly this moment.

It is the only large-scale financial asset in human history that cannot be diluted by committee, seized without keys, or destroyed by aerial bombardment. Its volatility, so often cited as a flaw, is simply the sound of price discovery in a world that has forgotten what sound money feels like. Every tremor is a reminder: risk is not the enemy of sovereignty; controllable counterparty is.

Tomorrow Powell will speak, and the price will gyrate. Some will declare the bull market over at the first sign of a weekly close below ninety thousand. There is only one asset that has spent sixteen years answering that question with perfect consistency.
