**The Silent Squeeze: BTC’s $87k Hold Amid Precious Metals’ Party**

## Context

Family’s unwrapping gifts, markets unwrapping pain—BTC’s rejected at $90k again, trading in that $85k-$90k cage like it’s waiting for permission to break free. Dominance creeping toward 58%, alts down 70% year-over-year, while gold smashes past $4,400 and silver tops $70. 

Kind of absurd, right? Echoes the dot-com hangover, where fundamentals built underground amid surface rot. Whales stacked 270k BTC in the last 30 days, the biggest haul in 13 years, even as sentiment hits rock bottom with the fear index at 27—extreme fear territory.

ETF flows tell the tale: BlackRock’s IBIT bled $157M yesterday, part of a $188M BTC ETF outflow streak, ETH shedding $95M too. Grayscale’s GBTC down $24.6M, marking four straight days of red. 

## Main

Tax-loss harvesting, holiday thinness... it’s the setup for volatility vampires. 

But here’s the irony—IBIT pulled in $25 billion this year despite BTC’s dip, ranking sixth in global ETF inflows. Institutions calling it a top 2025 theme alongside Magnificent 7 stocks and Treasury Bills. 

We bond over these pivots, that gut-twist when macros flip and you’re left holding the bag.

**Macro Shadows: Yields Dip, DXY Holds, and the Fed’s Lingering Cut Bets**

Ghosts of policy past never rest, even on holidays. 10-year yields hovering around 4.13%, DXY steady at 97.9—rumblings of data credibility issues with CPI at 2.7% versus expected 3.1%, GDP humming at 4.3%. 

But whispers point to over-optimism, AI fluff masking flat wages. Brrrr signals fading, risk assets shivering... yet gold’s the canary, up 1.11% intraday on re-accelerating inflation fears.

Fed odds? Markets baked in a 90% shot at a 25bps cut earlier this month, but “higher for longer” looms like bad eggnog. 

Equities ripping records, embarrassing crypto’s alt graveyard, while BTC’s correlation to Nasdaq tightens—yet decoupling risks brew. 

Contrarian edge: Bearish on frothy traps, but liquidity could juice dominance to 55%+ with probability. Long hard assets, short hype—could flip if demand stalls, but arithmetic leans resilient.

**Onchain Whispers: DAA Dips, TVL Slips, but Yield Plays Beckon**

Deeper in the trenches, onchain’s spilling truths over the eggnog. 

Daily active addresses dipped, hash rate holding steady but fees vanishing like holiday budgets. Total TVL at $118 billion, down 1.66% in 24 hours—no sharp 7-day rebound, Ethereum dominating while Bitcoin layers lag. Unlocks hanging like unwanted ornaments, pressuring alts further.

Wheels and perps grinding 35-60% APR on spots like Hyperliquid or Mezo—stake BTC, earn from fees in self-custody. Trench tale: Wheeled options through ‘24’s ETF hype, turning drawdowns into stacks. Abundance real, but infra gravity hits harder than FDV illusions. Vol bites, but onchain’s the alpha—eyes peeled, hunters.
