# 180242263.Fair Launch

## Metadata

**Source**: Substack Essay
**Category**: grinding-the-trenches
**Word Count**: 1,001
**Tags**: #eth #solana #sol #trading #substack

---

I’ve been in this space long enough to have lived through every single cycle of hope and disappointment. There was a time—call it 2016-2020 if you want—when “fair” actually meant something. You read a whitepaper, you hung out in Telegram, you talked to the devs, you threw money at something you believed in, and if you were right, you made life-changing money alongside the team. You weren’t just buying tokens. You were joining a movement. Even when the project died, you usually made friends who went on to build the next thing.

Then the VC meta ate everything.

## Context

Suddenly the game wasn’t “who can build the best thing” anymore.

It became “who can raise from the right funds the fastest.”

The whitepaper you were reading? Written after round 3.

## Main

The “community-first” sale? Priced at 5-10× the last private round.

The “locked team tokens”? Already OTC’d, borrowed against, or sitting in an unlabeled wallet doing god-knows-what.

Your conviction stopped mattering. All that mattered was whether you were on the cap table before the cap table even existed.

We told ourselves “next cycle will be different.”

It never was.

Every new “fair launch” platform that showed up in 2022-2024 turned out to be the same trick with better branding:

- “Invest alongside VCs!” → you just bought the last private round at 20× the seed price.

- “No pre-sale!” → unless you count the 300 undisclosed KOLs and the “strategic advisors” who all got 80% discounts.

- “Fully transparent vesting!” → one giant multisig labeled “Foundation Treasury” that starts dumping week 2.

I’m exhausted even typing this. We all are. And the X timeline? It’s a graveyard of threads calling out the same BS—VCs marking up spreadsheets while retail gets rugged, founders partying on “community” rounds that never launch, and “decentralized” platforms that are just syndication wrappers for the latest 9-figure valuation. Gigabrains are out here demoing previews and hyping the nostalgia, but even they know: without real enforcement, it’s all vaporware.

And yet… I still catch myself hoping someone, somewhere, will figure out how to bring back real fair launches. Launches where:

- You actually can be early.

- You can see 100% of the supply on-chain, in clearly labeled wallets.

- Locked really means programmatically locked, not “trust me bro.”

- Liquidity is pre-committed and locked at TGE so you’re not exit-liquidity for insiders.

- The team hasn’t already made 50x by selling OTC before you even heard the ticker.

I thought that was impossible in 2025. Turns out Jupiter has been quietly building exactly that, and the X hive mind is buzzing about it like it’s the spark we’ve been missing. They call it DTF—Decentralized Token Formation (or whatever cheeky meaning you prefer). From everything I’ve seen—and the threads dissecting it— it actually enforces the things we’ve all been begging for:

- 100% of supply disclosed and on-chain before the sale starts, with wallets labeled like a forensic audit.

- Lockups done with Jupiter’s own vesting contracts (no multisig magic).

- Liquidity pools pre-seeded and locked via Meteora at TGE—no more “illiquid hell” post-launch.

- First-come-first-served (FCFS), no tiers, no hidden allocations, no VC pre-pre-pre-sales. Even KOLs are joking about grinding in the trenches with the rest of us.

- They personally vet every project (and are willing to put their reputation behind it), starting with ones that align on bootstrapped vibes—no angels, no VCs, just builders.

The first one drops December 3: $WET by the HumidiFi team—the bootstrap monsters who built the biggest “dark AMM” on Solana with zero funding. In mere months, they’ve captured 47-50% of Jupiter’s routing market share, 30-40% of all SOL-USD volume (even out-trading Binance spot some weeks), and settled over $133B in total volume with just $10M TVL. Their secret? A prop AMM model that delivers CEX-level execution—sub-5 bps slippage, zero MEV via hidden orders, and oracle updates 17x per second—without a frontend, just pure aggregator routing. The team? Deep Solana roots—ex-MarginFi (30% overlap), builders of execution layers like Nozomi, investors in Backpack and Sanctum, and fresh off launching Harmonic for open block infra. No misaligned interests, just a crew proving they can scale to billions in volume solo.

No VC round. No angels. No KOL deals. This is, in many ways, a true ICO—an Initial Coin Offering—with ~$60M FDV for early/community allocations (including $JUP stakers) and ~$69M public sale, no caps, using USDC or SOL on Meteora pools. Whitelist via early Discord grind (now closed) or staking ≥50 SOL to Solar nodes by Nov 30 (top 40 snag $500 allocations). $JUP stakers? Discounted priority based on stake size/duration—unstake now, it’s just 7 days cliff. As the origin story goes, this is what happens when failure forces you to build something that actually solves real problems like stale prices and MEV. I don’t know if this fixes everything. I don’t know if DTF is the spark that brings back the magic—or if $WET moons to $AVICI-level multiples. But for the first time in years, the X chatter feels electric: threads breaking down metrics, hyping the no-whale FCFS, and even analysts calling it a “quiet bullish catalyst” with IP edge. I’m actually excited to open my wallet on launch day—not because I expect to get rich, but because I might finally get to be early to something I believe in again. No more paying VCs for the privilege.

## Conclusion

Maybe I’m an idiot for hoping. Or maybe, just maybe, Jupiter built the thing we’ve all been waiting for—and HumidiFi’s the perfect proof-of-concept to get us wet. We’ll find out together on December 3rd.

Oh, and one more thing. Yeah, Hyperliquid did pull off the fairest launch we’ve seen in years, with that monster airdrop that handed billions back to actual users, no VCs invited. But let’s be real: that magic only happened because the founders bootstrapped from their market-making days, flush enough to build a whole L1 without begging for pre-seed checks. Not every team has that war chest. DTF is for the rest of us—the dreamers who need a fair shot without needing to be crypto millionaires first.
