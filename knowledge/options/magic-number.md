How This Hedge Would Work

Your base: 3600 $HYPE selling $26 covered calls weekly for ~$2K premium (2% yield). Adding “aggressive” CSPs means selling puts at strikes deeper OTM or even ATM/ITM for higher delta (more sensitivity to dips)—e.g., $20-22 strike instead of your usual +2 OTM. This isn’t a full strangle (unlimited risk if naked), but secured with stables, so max loss is assignment (buying more at strike).

Weekly Routine:

Sell covered calls at +2 (~$27 this week on $25 spot) for upside income/hedge.

Sell CSPs at aggressive strike (e.g., $22, 12% OTM) with stables as collateral—aim for 1-2% premium per put contract.

If dip hits: Puts get assigned → buy more $HYPE cheaper, lowering your average cost (e.g., from $24 to $22.50 blended).

If no dip: Both expire worthless → double premiums (~$3-4K/week total), roll next week.

On rips: Calls might assign (sell at $27), but use put premiums or stables to rebuy via spot or new puts.

This creates a “collar-lite” effect: Calls cap upside mildly (but you rebuy if convicted), puts provide downside buffer via averaging.

Theta works double-time for you as seller on both sides.

Pros: Supercharges Your Yield and Conviction

Averaging Down on Dips: Perfect for $HYPE’s swingy nature—past year showed -8% average down weeks, so a 10-15% dip (to $21-22) triggers assignment, letting you scoop 1,000-2,000 more tokens at discount. Blended cost drops, setting up bigger wins on rebounds (e.g., to $30+ on ETF news).

Risk Alignment: Fits your low-allocation approach—no overexposure. If assigned on puts, you’re just deeper in a token you love (Hyperliquid’s 69% perp share, burns), not bag-holding junk. Rotates stables productively without touching BTC core.

Vol as Friend: $HYPE’s ~100-120% IV (inferred from your 118% APR) bloats put premiums more than calls in fear spikes, so dips pay you extra to buy low.

Cons and Risks: Capital Tie-Up and Assignment Overload

More Collateral Needed: CSPs require cash reserves (e.g., $20K per 1,000-token put at $20 strike).

Assignment Double-Edged: Big dip assigns puts → great for averaging (e.g., add 1,000 $HYPE at $20 net of premium, new average ~$23). But if dip persists (e.g., alt nuke), you’re over-allocated (stack grows 20-50%), amplifying drawdowns. Historical -20% weekly ranges mean this hits ~1-2x/quarter.

Opportunity Cost: Stables in CSPs earn yield but miss BTC upside (BTC’s been +2-5% weekly in bulls). If no dip, it’s “dead money” vs. spot holding.

Vol Crush or False Dips: Premiums shrink if IV fades post-unlock (February risk); choppy weeks could assign puts on wicks without real crash, tying capital unnecessarily.

Tax/Fees: On-chain, gas and slippage add up on rolls/assignments—minimal on Hypersurface, but track it.
