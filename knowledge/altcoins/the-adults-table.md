**The Persistent Misalignment Problem**

## Main

Since the ICO era, most crypto projects have separated equity (held by VCs and founders in a for-profit entity) from tokens (governance/utility for the public).

This creates perverse incentives: Company value accrues off-chain, while tokens rely on speculative demand or diluted voting. 

Critics rightly point out that this leaves token holders at the ‚Äúkids‚Äô table‚Äù‚Äîparticipating without meaningful ownership.

True equity grants‚Äîwhere token or NFT holders gain direct or indirect claims on company shares‚Äîare exceedingly rare due to regulatory hurdles (e.g., Howey Test risks classifying tokens as securities) and conflicts with VC interests.

Among the few attempts:

Rektguy NFTs (launched 2022): In 2024, Rekt Brands Inc. granted actual equity shares to holders via a Reg CF exemption (based on a 2023 snapshot). By 2025, the project thrives: Raised $1.5M seed funding, launched successful consumer products like Rekt Drinks (sell-outs globally), expanded into media/podcasts, and even issued a $REKT memecoin. NFT floor remains stable, proving community ownership can drive real-world brand success without implosion. No major ‚Äúrekt‚Äù examples exist from equity grants‚Äîthe regulatory moat has kept attempts limited and careful.

**Pioneers of True Alignment in 2025**

Two standout projects demonstrate viable paths forward:

- 
Rainbow Wallet ($RNBW): The Rainbow Foundation holds 20% of the company‚Äôs equity (via a special Class F share class) on behalf of token holders, making it the largest shareholder. This stake ensures holders share in upside from growth, acquisitions, or exits‚Äîproceeds distributed directly. Launched with a CoinList sale in late 2025 and TGE set for February 2026, Rainbow supplements this with real-time buybacks from platform earnings (swaps, perps). -

Nonprofit laws prevent profit distribution or sales, aligning incentives for long-term network growth. Founder Paul Frambot emphasizes: No separate winners‚Äîtoken holders capture the full upside.

These hybrids avoid pure securities classification while delivering economic rights closer to equity than governance alone.
